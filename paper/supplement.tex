\section{Model, convention, and cluster geometry}

We use local spin axes and
\begin{equation}
H=H_0+V=\Jzz\sum_{\langle ij\rangle}S_i^zS_j^z
-\Jpm\sum_{\langle ij\rangle}(S_i^+S_j^-+S_i^-S_j^+)
+\Jpp\sum_{\langle ij\rangle}(S_i^+S_j^++S_i^-S_j^-).
\label{eq:model_si}
\end{equation}
Huang \emph{et al.} use this same convention at the sign-free point
$(\Jpm,\Jpp)/\Jzz=(0.046,0)$~\cite{Huang2018}.  No sign or factor-of-two conversion is
therefore needed for the external benchmark.
For the diagonal local-axis form
$J_xS_i^xS_j^x+J_yS_i^yS_j^y+\Jzz S_i^zS_j^z$, our convention is
\begin{equation}
 \Jpm=-\frac{J_x+J_y}{4},\qquad
 \Jpp=\frac{J_x-J_y}{4}.
\end{equation}

Sites are represented in a periodic covering lattice.  For each nearest
neighbor pair $(i,j)$, the stored integer image $\bm n_{ij}$ gives the lifted
bond displacement
\begin{equation}
 \bm d_{ij}=\bm r_j-\bm r_i-\bm n_{ij}^{T}L,
\end{equation}
where the rows of $L$ are the three supercell vectors.  A depth-first cycle
enumeration retains simple cycles that preserve the ice constraint and, for
six-cycles, are planar in the covering lattice.  The resulting census is
listed in Table~\ref{tab:cluster_census}.

\section{Ice-rule projector and microscopic source}

Define the tetrahedron charge $\widehat Q_t=\sum_{i\in t}S_i^z$.  Since the
charges commute, they have a common $S^z$ basis.  The symbol
$\mathbf 1_{\{0\}}(\widehat Q_t)$ means the projector onto the zero-charge
eigenspace of tetrahedron $t$:
\begin{equation}
 \mathbf 1_{\{0\}}(\widehat Q_t)|s\rangle=
 \begin{cases}
  |s\rangle,&Q_t(s)=0,\\
  0,&Q_t(s)\ne0.
 \end{cases}
\label{eq:local_ice_projector_si}
\end{equation}
For spin $1/2$, $Q_t\in\{-2,-1,0,1,2\}$, so the same local projector has the
explicit polynomial form
\begin{equation}
 \mathbf 1_{\{0\}}(\widehat Q_t)
 =(1-\widehat Q_t^2)(1-\widehat Q_t^2/4).
\end{equation}
Multiplying these commuting local projectors imposes zero charge on every
tetrahedron:
\begin{equation}
 \Pice=\prod_t\mathbf 1_{\{0\}}(\widehat Q_t)
 =\sum_{\substack{s:\,Q_t(s)=0\\\text{for every }t}}|s\rangle\langle s|.
\label{eq:ice_projector_si}
\end{equation}
This definition is independent of cluster size and of any enumeration of ice
configurations.  The operator is Hermitian and idempotent,
$\Pice^\dagger=\Pice$ and $\Pice^2=\Pice$, and projects onto the full subspace
satisfying the ice rule on every tetrahedron.

For an oriented bond $(i,j)$, the stored image $\bm n_{ij}$ defines
\begin{equation}
 \bm d_{ij}=\bm r_j-\bm r_i-\bm n_{ij}^{T}L,
 \qquad
 \bm c_{ij}=\bm r_i+\bm r_j-\bm n_{ij}^{T}L.
\end{equation}
This second coordinate is fixed, rather than chosen as an additional source
convention.  Write the lifted position of the second site as
$\widetilde{\bm r}_j=\bm r_j-\bm n_{ij}^{T}L$.  Since $S^+$ changes $S^z$
by $+1$, a double-raising move changes the lifted spin polarization by
\begin{equation}
 \Delta\bm p(S_i^+S_j^+)=\bm r_i+\widetilde{\bm r}_j=\bm c_{ij},
\end{equation}
whereas double lowering gives $-\bm c_{ij}$.  Similarly,
$S_i^+S_j^-$ gives $\bm r_i-\widetilde{\bm r}_j=-\bm d_{ij}$.  Attaching
$e^{-2i\btheta\cdot\Delta\bm p}$ to each matrix element therefore produces
all four phases below from one polarization-counting rule.
The source is inserted directly into the microscopic exchange terms:
\begin{align}
 H(\btheta)=H_0-\Jpm\sum_{\langle ij\rangle}
 \left[e^{+2i\btheta\cdot\bm d_{ij}}S_i^+S_j^-
      +e^{-2i\btheta\cdot\bm d_{ij}}S_i^-S_j^+\right]\nonumber\\
 {}+\Jpp\sum_{\langle ij\rangle}
 \left[e^{-2i\btheta\cdot\bm c_{ij}}S_i^+S_j^+
      +e^{+2i\btheta\cdot\bm c_{ij}}S_i^-S_j^-\right].
\label{eq:sourced_si}
\end{align}
Equation~(\ref{eq:sourced_si}) is an exact microscopic finite-cluster
Hamiltonian.  No low-energy reduction, energy denominator, or expansion in
$\Jpm/\Jzz$ or $\Jpp/\Jzz$ has been used.
For a basis state with $i$ down and $j$ up, the corresponding off-diagonal
matrix element is
\begin{equation}
 \langle S_i^+S_j^-s|H(\btheta)|s\rangle
 =-\Jpm e^{+2i\btheta\cdot\bm d_{ij}},
\end{equation}
and the reverse exchange carries the complex-conjugate phase.  This is the
complete mathematical meaning of ``attaching a microscopic source.''  For
example, if both spins are down,
\begin{equation}
 \langle S_i^+S_j^+s|H(\btheta)|s\rangle
 =\Jpp e^{-2i\btheta\cdot\bm c_{ij}},
\end{equation}
and the double-lowering move again carries the complex conjugate.
Unlike the $\Jpm$ exchange, the $\Jpp$ pair flip changes total $S^z$ by two.
The cubic-16 implementation therefore uses the complete $2^{16}$-state basis
when $\Jpp\ne0$; the $12{,}870$-state $S^z=0$ block is used only on the
$\Jpp=0$ benchmark slice.  The code raises an error if a nonzero pair-flip
term is requested in a basis that is not closed under it.

For a sequence $\gamma$ of microscopic moves, step $\ell$ acts on the stored
oriented bond $(i_\ell,j_\ell)$.  Define its lifted polarization increment
\begin{equation}
 \Delta\bm p_\ell=\begin{cases}
 -\bm d_{ij},&T_\ell=S_i^+S_j^-,\\
 +\bm d_{ij},&T_\ell=S_i^-S_j^+,\\
 +\bm c_{ij},&T_\ell=S_i^+S_j^+,\\
 -\bm c_{ij},&T_\ell=S_i^-S_j^-.
 \end{cases}
\label{eq:polarization_increment_si}
\end{equation}
The product of source phases is
\begin{equation}
 \prod_{\ell\in\gamma}e^{-2i\btheta\cdot\Delta\bm p_\ell}
 =e^{-i\btheta\cdot\bq_\gamma},
 \qquad
 \bq_\gamma=2\sum_{\ell\in\gamma}\Delta\bm p_\ell.
\label{eq:path_phase_si}
\end{equation}
With the primitive coordinate convention used in the code, every completed
ice-to-ice sequence has $\bq_\gamma\in\mathbb Z^3$.  Contractible paths have
$\bq_\gamma=0$; the targeted paths carrying nonzero net transport through a
periodic image have $\bq_\gamma\ne0$.  A change of home-cell representatives
redistributes phase between bond increments and basis states but does not
change the completed transport or its vanishing.
Equation~(\ref{eq:path_phase_si}) follows only by multiplying the microscopic
phases and is exact for every exchange sequence; the path notation does not
invoke perturbation theory.

The continuous character average of a completed path is consequently
\begin{equation}
 \frac{1}{(2\pi)^3}\int_{[0,2\pi)^3}d^3\theta\,
 e^{-i\btheta\cdot\bq_\gamma}
 =\delta_{\bq_\gamma,\bm0}.
\label{eq:path_selector_si}
\end{equation}
Thus a finite-cell process that closes with nonzero lifted polarization
transport has exactly zero winding-free coefficient.  A contractible process,
including any legitimate pair-flip correction with zero total transport, is
retained.  On an $M^3$ grid the right-hand side is instead one for
$\bq_\gamma=\bm0\pmod M$, which is the finite-$M$ alias discussed below.

For a concrete mixed-channel example, use the cubic-16 site convention in the
code and apply $S_0^-S_1^+$, $S_8^+S_{10}^+$, and
$S_8^-S_{11}^-$.  The three lifted increments are
\begin{equation}
 \left(0,\frac14,\frac14\right),\qquad
 \left(\frac54,0,\frac54\right),\qquad
 \left(-\frac54,-\frac14,-1\right).
\end{equation}
The sequence connects two ice configurations but has
$\bq_\gamma=2\sum_\ell\Delta\bm p_\ell=(0,0,1)$, so
Eq.~(\ref{eq:path_selector_si}) removes this spurious
$\Jpm\Jpp^2$ four-spin contribution.  In contrast, applying a pair flip and
its inverse gives increments $-\bm c_{ij}$ and $+\bm c_{ij}$, hence
$\bq_\gamma=0$; that diagonal backtracking correction is not removed.

Crucially, the protocol does not average the microscopic
$H(\btheta)$ itself.  It first obtains the exact source-dependent band and its
canonical pullback $h(\btheta)$, then averages that operator.  Every term in a
resolvent expansion inherits the phase product above, while the exact Fourier
series of $h(\btheta)$ collects those terms to all orders.  Character
orthogonality therefore removes the nonzero-transport Fourier coefficients
without deleting the microscopic moves needed to build zero-transport paths.

\section{Exact band projector and canonical pullback}

The two projectors in the construction have different roles.  $\Pice$ is the
fixed reference projector in Eq.~(\ref{eq:ice_projector_si}).  By contrast,
$P_{\btheta}$ is the exact spectral projector of $H(\btheta)$ onto the band
continuously connected to the ice-rule subspace as the transverse
exchange is turned on.  If $\Gamma_{\btheta}$ encloses this isolated band and
no other spectrum,
\begin{equation}
 P_{\btheta}=\frac{1}{2\pi i}\oint_{\Gamma_{\btheta}}
 (z-H(\btheta))^{-1}dz
 =\sum_{\alpha\in\mathcal B_{\btheta}}
 |\psi_{\alpha\btheta}\rangle\langle\psi_{\alpha\btheta}|.
\label{eq:spectral_projector_si}
\end{equation}
Its rank equals $\operatorname{rank}\Pice$.  The protocol is undefined if the
band touches its complement during the continuation.

The positive operator
$G_{\btheta}=\Pice P_{\btheta}\Pice$ acts on
the ice-rule subspace.  If it is nonsingular, the polar partial isometry
\begin{equation}
 W_{\btheta}=P_{\btheta}\Pice G_{\btheta}^{-1/2}
\label{eq:polar_si}
\end{equation}
satisfies $W_{\btheta}^\dagger W_{\btheta}=\Pice$ and
$W_{\btheta}W_{\btheta}^\dagger=P_{\btheta}$.  It fixes the otherwise
arbitrary eigenvector gauge within the exact band.  The operator represented
in the common ice-rule reference space is
\begin{equation}
 h(\btheta)=W_{\btheta}^\dagger H(\btheta)W_{\btheta}.
\label{eq:band_si}
\end{equation}

For the numerical implementation, let $B$ contain an orthonormal basis of
the ice-rule subspace, and let $\Psi_{\btheta}$ and $E_{\btheta}$ contain
the exact band eigenvectors and eigenvalues.  Setting
\begin{equation}
 X_{\btheta}=B^\dagger\Psi_{\btheta},\qquad
 U_{\btheta}=X_{\btheta}(X_{\btheta}^\dagger X_{\btheta})^{-1/2}
\end{equation}
gives the matrix
\begin{equation}
 h(\btheta)=U_{\btheta}\operatorname{diag}(E_{\btheta})U_{\btheta}^\dagger.
\label{eq:numerical_band_si}
\end{equation}
The minimum eigenvalue of $X_{\btheta}^\dagger X_{\btheta}$ tests the polar
overlap.  A singular Gram matrix is a failure of the selected-band
identification, not a numerical singularity to regularize away.

\section{Character projection and its nonperturbative content}

The exact pulled-back operator has the transport Fourier series
\begin{equation}
 h(\btheta)=\sum_{\bq\in\mathbb Z^3}h_{\bq}
 e^{-i\btheta\cdot\bq}.
\label{eq:fourier_si}
\end{equation}
Its winding-free, zero-transport component is
\begin{equation}
 h_{\bq=0}=\frac{1}{(2\pi)^3}\int_{[0,2\pi)^3}
 d^3\theta\,h(\btheta).
\label{eq:continuous_si}
\end{equation}
The implemented $M^3$ character rule is
\begin{equation}
 h_M=\frac{1}{M^3}\sum_{\bm m\in\mathbb Z_M^3}
 h(2\pi\bm m/M)
 =\sum_{\bq\in M\mathbb Z^3}h_{\bq}.
\label{eq:alias}
\end{equation}
The last equality follows from discrete Fourier orthogonality.  A finite grid
aliases nonzero transports $M\bm k$, so convergence in $M$ is mandatory.
In particular, $M=2$ is only a parity selector: it retains every Fourier
component with $\bq=2\bm k$.  It cannot by itself establish zero transport.

This construction is nonperturbative for the selected finite-cluster band in
a precise sense.  At every $\btheta$, the code diagonalizes the microscopic
$H(\btheta)$ at the target $(\Jpm,\Jpp)/\Jzz$ and evaluates
$P_{\btheta}$, $G_{\btheta}^{-1/2}$, and $h(\btheta)$ without expanding in the
coupling.  If one introduces a formal parameter $\lambda$, analyticity of the
isolated band would give
\begin{equation}
 h(\btheta;\lambda)=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\lambda^n
 \sum_{\bq}h_{n,\bq}e^{-i\btheta\cdot\bq},
\end{equation}
and Eq.~(\ref{eq:continuous_si}) would return
$\sum_n\lambda^nh_{n,0}$.  The production calculation evaluates the exact
left-hand side at $\lambda=1$ instead of truncating the series on the right.

The claim has explicit boundaries: exact diagonalization is for a finite
Hilbert space and chosen symmetry sector; the character integral is reached
only after $M$ convergence; and the isolated-band gap and polar overlap must
remain open.  None of these conditions implies cluster-size convergence.

The order of operations cannot be changed.  Averaging $H(\btheta)$ before
diagonalization removes individual hops before they compose.  Averaging
$\operatorname{Tr}e^{-\beta H(\btheta)}$ averages a nonlinear observable,
not the operator in Eq.~(\ref{eq:continuous_si}).  Likewise, a counterterm
guessed before the character projection would cancel selected amplitudes
rather than transport sectors.  Equation~(\ref{eq:full_hamiltonian_si}) instead
embeds the correction only after the exact character projection is complete.

\section{Perturbative path enumeration as a topology audit}

Perturbation theory is not used to construct the production spectrum.  It is
used to verify Eq.~(\ref{eq:path_phase_si}) on the shortest known processes.
Let $Q_{\rm ice}=1-\Pice$ and
\begin{equation}
 R=-Q_{\rm ice}(H_0-E_{\rm ice})^{-1}Q_{\rm ice}.
\end{equation}
Because $\Pice V\Pice=0$, the diagnostic rows at second and third order are
\begin{align}
 h^{(2)}&=\Pice VRV\Pice,\\
 h^{(3)}&=\Pice VRVRV\Pice.
\end{align}
The code stores each virtual sequence and merges only rows with the same source
ice state $s_r$, target ice state $t_r$, and covering-lattice image transport.
For every off-diagonal row $r$, the bit mask $s_r\mathbin\oplus t_r$ identifies
the spins flipped by the completed sequence.  This mask is matched against the
independently enumerated ice-preserving four- and six-cycles.  Summing the
stored bond images around that cycle classifies it as contractible or wrapping;
Eq.~(\ref{eq:path_phase_si}) independently gives its transported dipole
$\bq_r$.

For a channel $\mathcal A$, define a distinct term by its spin-flip support
$\mu_r=s_r\mathbin\oplus t_r$.  The number of such terms retained by an
$M^3$ character grid is
\begin{equation}
 N_{\mathcal A}^{(M)}=
 \left|\left\{\mu_r:\ r\in\mathcal A,\ c_r\ne0,\
 \bq_r=\bm0\pmod M\right\}\right|.
\label{eq:channel_count_si}
\end{equation}
Here $c_r$ includes the coherent virtual-path sum at fixed
$(s_r,t_r,\text{image})$, and $M=1$ is the unprojected census.  The
independently generated loop census and row audit give

Table~\ref{tab:path_retention} counts terms of the perturbative diagnostic.
The same classification can be applied directly to the production band
operators, which is the stronger statement quoted in the main text.  Both
$h(\bm0)$ and $h_M$ are matrices in the fixed reference space spanned by the
$d=90$ zero-spinon configurations of cubic-16.  Let $\mu$ be the spin-flip
support of an enumerated loop.  Every ordered pair of zero-spinon
configurations $(s,t)$ with $t=s\oplus\mu$ then samples that loop's amplitude
in a produced operator, and
\begin{equation}
 A_{\mathcal A}[h]=
 \operatorname{mean}\left\{\left|h_{st}\right|:\

\label{eq:loop_amplitude_si}

enters Eq.~(\ref{eq:loop_amplitude_si}): $h$ is the polar pullback of the

Four features are worth stating explicitly.  First, in the periodic band the
winding four-loop amplitude exceeds the contractible hexagon amplitude by a
factor of $8.0$, so the leading ice-to-ice process of ordinary exact
diagonalization on this cluster is the artifact rather than the ring
exchange.  Second, at the production grid $M=4$ the winding amplitudes are
$\le1.2\times10^{-16}\Jzz$, fourteen orders of magnitude below $g_4$ and at
the arithmetic precision of the average; the character projection alone
achieves this, independently of the subsequent $\bm p$-block projection.
Third, the odd grid $M=3$ leaves a residual winding amplitude of
$1.1\times10^{-6}\Jzz$.  This is four orders of magnitude below $g_6$ and
consistent with the $0.013\%$ centered operator agreement between $M=3$ and
$M=4$ in Eq.~(\ref{eq:centered_change_si}), but it is not at arithmetic
precision, and it is a further reason to quote $M=4$ as the production grid.
Fourth, the contractible hexagon amplitude changes by only $0.8\%$ between
$h(\bm0)$ and $h_{M=4}$, and sits $13\%$ above the bare third-order value
$g_6$.  That excess is the higher-order virtual dressing of the physical ring
exchange, which the exact-band construction retains and a truncated
third-order effective model would discard.

For reference, canonical Schrieffer--Wolff theory~\cite{Bravyi2011} generates
the Taylor coefficients $h_{n,\bq}$ when the band is analytic.  Agreement
with the exact-band source dependence at weak coupling is a useful audit, but
no finite Schrieffer--Wolff order appears in the production definition.

To quantify character-grid convergence without counting a uniform shift of
the selected band as a physical change, define
\begin{align}
 \widetilde h_M&=h_M-\frac{\operatorname{Tr}h_M}{d}\,I_d,\\
 \epsilon_{M-1\to M}&=
 \frac{\|\widetilde h_{M-1}-\widetilde h_M\|_{\mathrm F}}
      {\|\widetilde h_M\|_{\mathrm F}},
\label{eq:centered_change_si}
\end{align}
where $d=\operatorname{rank}\Pice=90$ for cubic-16 and
$\|A\|_{\mathrm F}=[\operatorname{Tr}(A^\dagger A)]^{1/2}$.  ``Exact'' here
means that every $h_M$ is averaged from the exact microscopic eigenspaces and
their polar pullbacks; it does not mean that a finite $M$ equals the continuous
character integral.  Centering removes only the identity component, which
changes no level spacing, heat capacity, or spectral transition frequency
within the band.

One consequence of the continuous character integral is available exactly at
finite $M$.  Because $\bq_\gamma=2\Delta\bm p$, a matrix element that survives
the continuous average cannot change the polarization coordinate, so
$h_{\rm wf}$ is block diagonal in $\bm p$.  We therefore impose that block
structure on $h_M$, discarding the inter-sector elements that a finite grid
retains through $\bq_\gamma=\bm0\pmod M$.  On cubic-16 the discarded coupling
is $1.6\times10^{-5}\Jzz$ at $M=4$.  This is small in operator norm but not in
its consequences: left in place, it splits the sixfold ground-state manifold
by $3.5\times10^{-5}\Jzz$, which introduces a spurious Schottky peak at
$T/\Jzz\simeq1.5\times10^{-5}$, drives the residual entropy from $\ln6$ down
to $\ln3$ below that temperature, and moves spectral weight to
$\omega\simeq0$ at $\Gamma$.  The splitting tracks the discarded coupling
across $M$ and is not a floating-point effect: states within each degenerate
multiplet agree to $4\times10^{-16}$, eleven orders of magnitude below it.
Imposing the block structure is exact at any $M$ and removes all three
artifacts.

At $(\Jpm,\Jpp)/\Jzz=(0.046,0)$,
Eq.~(\ref{eq:centered_change_si}) gives 6.36\% from
$M=2$ to 3 and 0.013\% from $M=3$ to 4, as shown in
Fig.~\ref{fig:character_convergence}.  The latter passes the 5\%
character-convergence gate.  The zero-source minimum ice overlap is 0.762,
and every generic $M=3,4$ point has a larger overlap.  These grids are
successive character resolutions: $M=3$ removes the parity alias left by
$M=2$, while comparison with the independent $M=4$ grid tests the remaining
finite-$M$ aliases.

\section{Full-temperature thermodynamics}

The final method is defined on the complete microscopic Hilbert space, not on
the ice reference space.  The projectors construct its counterterm; they do
not restrict the thermodynamic trace.  Set $d=\operatorname{rank}\Pice$ and fix the
otherwise undetermined scalar placement of the corrected band by
\begin{equation}
 \bar h_M=h_M+\frac{\operatorname{Tr}[h(\bm0)-h_M]}{d}I_d.
\label{eq:trace_match_si}
\end{equation}
The full-Hilbert winding-free Hamiltonian is
\begin{equation}
 H_{\rm wf}^{(M)}=H(\bm0)+W_0[\bar h_M-h(\bm0)]W_0^\dagger.
\label{eq:full_hamiltonian_si}
\end{equation}
It is one temperature-independent Hermitian operator.  Since
$W_0W_0^\dagger=P_0$, it equals $H(\bm0)$ exactly on the spectral complement
of the ice-descended band.  The correction therefore acts in the microscopic
Hilbert space but is generally a nonlocal, band-supported counterterm;
the low-order projected rows only verify its loop content.

The partition function is the ordinary full trace
\begin{equation}
 Z_{\rm wf}=\operatorname{Tr}_{\mathcal H}e^{-\beta H_{\rm wf}^{(M)}}.
\label{eq:full_partition_si}
\end{equation}
For computation, let $\{E_i\}$ be the full periodic spectrum, $B$ the indices
of its selected band, and $\{\widetilde E_a\}$ the eigenvalues of $\bar h_M$.
The exact spectral decomposition of Eq.~(\ref{eq:full_partition_si}) gives,
for $k=0,1,2$,
\begin{equation}
 M_k^{\rm wf}(\beta)=
 \sum_{i\notin B}E_i^k e^{-\beta E_i}
 +\sum_a\widetilde E_a^k e^{-\beta\widetilde E_a}.
\label{eq:moments}
\end{equation}
Then
\begin{align}
 E&=M_1/M_0,\\
 C&=\beta^2\left(M_2/M_0-(M_1/M_0)^2\right),\\
 S&=\ln M_0+\beta E.
\end{align}
Equation~(\ref{eq:moments}) is thus an evaluation identity for the full
Hamiltonian in Eq.~(\ref{eq:full_hamiltonian_si}), not a separate
thermodynamic prescription.
For the present $\Jpp=0$ cubic-16 benchmark we use the complete
65,536-level spectrum.  For FCC-32 the same
identity requires stochastic trace estimation of the complement; that
calculation has not yet been performed.

\section{Longitudinal winding-free dynamics}

The full response is defined using $H_{\rm wf}^{(M)}$ and the unchanged
microscopic spin operator.  The plotted range lies below the minimum
$0.440\Jzz$ excitation into the exact band complement, so no complement state
can occur as a final state in that window.  The full Lehmann representation
therefore reduces exactly to the following band restriction.  The
microscopic spin probe is pulled back with the exact zero-source canonical map.
Let $W_0$ denote Eq.~(\ref{eq:polar_si}) at $\btheta=0$ and define
\begin{equation}
 \widetilde S_a^z(\bq)=W_0^\dagger
 \left(\sum_{j\in a}e^{-2\pi i\bq\cdot\bm r_j}S_j^z\right)W_0,
\label{eq:dssf_source_si}
\end{equation}
where $a=0,\ldots,3$ labels the four pyrochlore sublattices.  We use the same
$\widetilde S_a^z$ with the periodic operator $h(\bm0)$ and the winding-free
operator $h_{M=4}$.  This avoids attributing a change of probe convention to
the winding-free Hamiltonian.

For the low-energy eigenstates of either full Hamiltonian,
\begin{equation}
 S^{zz}(\bq,\omega)=\frac{1}{Nd_0}
 \sum_{g\in\mathcal G}\sum_{n\notin\mathcal G}\sum_{a=0}^{3}
 |\langle n|\widetilde S_a^z(\bq)|g\rangle|^2
 G_\eta[\omega-(E_n-E_g)],
\label{eq:dssf_si}
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal G$ contains the states degenerate with the numerical ground
state to $10^{-9}\Jzz$, $d_0=|\mathcal G|$, and $G_\eta$ is a normalized
Gaussian with $\eta=6\times10^{-4}\Jzz$.  Equation~(\ref{eq:dssf_si}) is the
four-sublattice trace of the longitudinal pseudospin response.  It is not an
unpolarized neutron cross section and includes no local-axis projection,
magnetic form factor, or transverse channel.

The four characters of the cubic-16 translation group are represented by
\begin{equation}
 \bq=\Gamma=(0,0,0),\quad X_x=(1,0,0),\quad
 X_y=(0,1,0),\quad X_z=(0,0,1)
\end{equation}
in reciprocal cubic units.  No interpolated momenta are plotted.  The complete
line summary is given in Table~\ref{tab:dssf_lines}.

The three winding-free $X$ channels agree to $5\times10^{-16}$ in integrated
weight.  The vanishing of the $\Gamma$ response is exact, not numerical.  At
$\bm k=\bm 0$ the probe is the sublattice magnetization
$M_a=\sum_{j\in a}S^z_j$, and on the ice manifold $M_a$ takes a single value on
each polarization sector: we find the variation of $M_a$ within a sector to be
identically zero, both on cubic-16 (90 ice states, 25 sectors) and on FCC-32
(2970 ice states, 85 sectors).  Since the winding-free Hamiltonian is block
diagonal in $\bm p$, its eigenvectors lie in a single sector, on which $M_a$
acts as a c-number and therefore has no off-diagonal element.  Evaluated with

stored in \texttt{campaign/outputs/dssf\_cubic16\_p0p046000.npz} and the
