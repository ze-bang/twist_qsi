\title{Unbiased exact diagonalization of quantum spin ice in the
$\pi$-flux regime:\\
removing finite-size contamination by translationally-invariant
twist averaging}
\author{}
\date{\today}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
The thermodynamics and dynamics of quantum spin ice (QSI) are governed
by an emergent compact $U(1)$ gauge field. Its unfrustrated
\emph{$0$-flux} sector ($\Jpm>0$) is accessible to sign-problem-free
quantum Monte Carlo (QMC); the frustrated \emph{$\pi$-flux} sector
($\Jpm<0$) realized in the dipolar--octupolar cerium pyrochlores carries
a QMC sign problem, leaving exact diagonalization (ED) as the only
unbiased finite-temperature method. ED has a finite-size problem
specific to QSI: a periodic cluster hosts short ice-preserving ring
exchanges that wind the torus and have no infinite-lattice analogue. The
dominant ones are four-site loops with coupling $\gfour=4\Jpm^2/\Jzz$,
which is \emph{second} order in $\Jpm$ and hence parametrically larger
than the genuine photon coupling $\ghex=12|\Jpm|^3/\Jzz^2$; they
contaminate exactly the emergent-gauge observables (the low-$T$
specific-heat peak and the low-energy $S^{zz}(\bq,\omega)$). We remove
this contamination by \emph{twist averaging in the
translationally-invariant gauge}: threading a $U(1)$ flux through the
cluster as a uniform vector potential on \emph{every} bond and averaging
the effective Hamiltonian over the flux. We show, mathematically and by
direct computation, that this average annihilates every winding ring
exchange while preserving every contractible one -- provided two
conditions are met that the naive procedure violates: the flux must be
distributed uniformly (the boundary-only gauge removes merely half), and
the average must span the doubled period set by the half-integer
transport of the emergent charges (the naive $2\pi$ period leaves a
$40\%$ residual). Both conditions have a transparent origin: a winding
ring exchange transports emergent charge across the cluster, and only a
translationally-invariant flux, averaged over its true period, dephases
that transport completely. On the $16$- and $32$-site clusters the
method moves the low-$T$ gauge peak and the low-energy $S^{zz}$ weight
from the artifact scale $\gfour$ to the photon scale $\ghex$ for both
flux signs, while leaving the high-$T$ charge (spinon) peak -- genuine,
uncontaminated physics -- untouched. This makes a $32$-site pyrochlore
an unbiased laboratory for the $\pi$-flux gauge sector, to be
benchmarked against $0$-flux QMC and applied at the exchange parameters
of the cerium pyrochlores.
\end{abstract}

\section{The problem}
\label{sec:problem}

\paragraph{Quantum spin ice and its emergent photon.}
The minimal model of QSI is the XXZ antiferromagnet on the pyrochlore
lattice, in the local $\langle111\rangle$ frames,
\begin{equation}
H=\Jzz\sum_{\langle ij\rangle}S^z_iS^z_j
-\Jpm\sum_{\langle ij\rangle}\bigl(S^+_iS^-_j+S^-_iS^+_j\bigr),
\qquad 0<|\Jpm|\ll\Jzz .
\label{eq:H}
\end{equation}
For $\Jpm\to0$ the ground manifold is the exponentially degenerate set
of $\twoinout$ (ice-rule) states. A small $\Jpm$ lifts the degeneracy
through virtual ring exchanges and generates the deconfined $U(1)$
lattice gauge theory of QSI: $S^z$ is the emergent electric field, the
third-order hexagonal ring exchange
\begin{equation}
\ghex=\frac{12\,|\Jpm|^3}{\Jzz^2}
\label{eq:ghex}
\end{equation}
is the Maxwell term whose quantum is the emergent photon, and ice-rule
defects are gapped, fractionalized spinons (pair threshold
$\Ds\approx0.8\,\Jzz$ on the clusters below). The \emph{sign} of the
ring coupling follows $\mathrm{sgn}(\Jpm^3)$: $\Jpm>0$ gives the
unfrustrated $0$-flux vacuum, $\Jpm<0$ the frustrated $\pi$-flux vacuum.
Thermodynamically the photon appears as a low-$T$ Schottky peak in
$C(T)$ at $T\sim\ghex$, far below the spinon (charge) peak at
$T\sim\Jzz$; dynamically it appears as low-energy weight in the
non-spin-flip channel $S^{zz}(\bq,\omega)$.

\paragraph{Why $\pi$-flux needs exact diagonalization.}
In the $S^z$ basis the off-diagonal matrix elements of \cref{eq:H} are
$-\Jpm$. For $\Jpm>0$ they are negative and worldline/stochastic-series
QMC is sign-problem-free: the $0$-flux QSI is quantitatively
characterized. For $\Jpm<0$ they are positive and QMC acquires a sign
problem. The $\pi$-flux regime -- believed to describe
Ce$_2$Zr$_2$O$_7$, Ce$_2$Sn$_2$O$_7$, Ce$_2$Hf$_2$O$_7$ -- is thus a QMC
dead zone, and unbiased finite-$T$ information must come from exact
diagonalization of a finite cluster.

\paragraph{The finite-size problem.}
A periodic cluster is a flat torus, and its periodic identifications
generate ice-preserving ring exchanges that \emph{wind} the torus with
no counterpart on the infinite lattice. The shortest and most damaging
are \emph{four-site} loops. Because a four-site loop factorizes into two
disjoint nearest-neighbour (NN) hops, it is generated at \emph{second}
order in $\Jpm$,
\begin{equation}
\gfour=\frac{4\,\Jpm^2}{\Jzz},
\qquad\text{so}\qquad
\frac{\gfour}{\ghex}=\frac{\Jzz}{3|\Jpm|}\gg1 .
\label{eq:g4}
\end{equation}
The artifact is thus \emph{parametrically larger} than the physics, and
grows as $|\Jpm|\to0$ -- the weak-coupling regime of the cerium
materials. It lives entirely in the ice manifold, so it contaminates
precisely the emergent-gauge observables: it pushes the low-$T$ $C(T)$
peak and the low-energy $S^{zz}$ weight up to the scale $\gfour$ instead
of $\ghex$ (quantified in \cref{sec:demo}). The spinon continuum
($S^{+-}$ at $\omega\sim\Jzz$) lies outside the ice manifold and is
\emph{not} contaminated.

\paragraph{Goal.}
Remove this finite-size contamination from the gauge sector of a
periodic-cluster ED, so that the $32$-site pyrochlore delivers unbiased
$\pi$-flux thermodynamics and dynamical structure factor.

\section{The effective gauge Hamiltonian and the winding loops}
\label{sec:setup}

\paragraph{Cluster torus.}
The cluster carries $N$ sites with periodic identifications generated by
the rows of an integer matrix $\bL\in\Z^{3\times3}$ (in units of the
cubic lattice constant $a$); position space is the flat torus
$T^3=\mathbb R^3/\bL\Z^3$ with first homology $\Z^3$. Each NN bond
$(i,j)$ has a \emph{wrap vector}
$\bn_{ij}=\mathrm{round}[(\br_j-\br_i)\bL^{-1}]$, zero in the interior
and nonzero across the boundary.

\paragraph{Effective Hamiltonian.}
Degenerate ($\Jpm$-)perturbation theory in the ice manifold yields
$\Heff=\sum_{\mathcal C}c_{\mathcal C}O_{\mathcal C}$, a sum of
ring-exchange operators
$O_{\mathcal C}=(S^+_aS^-_bS^+_cS^-_d\cdots)$ over ice-preserving cycles
$\mathcal C$; because each intermediate resolvent is Ising
($\propto\Jzz$), $c_{\mathcal C}\propto\Jpm^{|\mathcal C|/2}$ exactly. On
the $2{\times}2{\times}2$ FCC cluster ($N=32$) the ice manifold has
dimension $2970$, so $\Heff$ is \emph{fully diagonalizable} and its
thermodynamics is numerically exact. Enumeration (Schrieffer--Wolff with
per-process bookkeeping) gives \cref{tab:census}: the winding four-loops
and wrapping hexagons are the contamination; the contractible hexagons
carry the photon.

\section{The method: twist averaging in the translationally-invariant gauge}
\label{sec:method}

\paragraph{Twist as a flat $U(1)$ connection.}
Thread a $U(1)$ flux $\bvarphi\in[0,2\pi)^3$ through the three
non-contractible cycles by attaching a phase to each transverse hop. The
gauge-invariant content of the flux is its holonomy $e^{-i\bw\cdot\bvarphi}$
around a loop of winding $\bw$, but \emph{how} the phase is distributed
over bonds is a gauge choice, and the choice matters for the average
below. We use the \emph{translationally-invariant} (``smooth'') gauge --
a uniform vector potential $\bA=\bL^{-1}\bvarphi$ that places
$\theta_{ij}=\bA\cdot\bd_{ij}$ on \emph{every} bond, with $\bd_{ij}$ the
minimum-image bond displacement:
\begin{equation}
\Heff(\bvarphi):\quad
c_{\mathcal C}\;\to\;c_{\mathcal C}\,e^{-i\,\bA\cdot\bdelta_{\mathcal C}},
\qquad
\bdelta_{\mathcal C}=\sum_{\text{hops}}\bd
=\underbrace{\sum_{i\in R}\br_i-\sum_{j\in L}\br_j}_{\brho}\;+\;\bN\!\cdot\!\bL ,
\label{eq:phase}
\end{equation}
where $\bdelta_{\mathcal C}$ is the \emph{net charge transport} of the
process -- the physical displacement of the moved $S^z$ (boson) density,
$\brho$ its intrinsic dipole and $\bN\!\cdot\!\bL$ the wrap contribution.
Thus in the smooth gauge each ring exchange is weighted by a phase set
by how far it transports emergent charge across the cluster
(\cref{fig:transport}).

\begin{figure}[t]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figs/fig_transport.pdf}
\caption{Charge transport on the $16$-site cubic pyrochlore cell,
$\bdelta=\sum_{\rm raised}\br-\sum_{\rm lowered}\br$. (a) A contractible
hexagon (sites $0,2,7,4,14,15$) is balanced -- the raised ($+$) and
lowered ($-$) sites share a centroid -- so $\bdelta=\bm0$ and it is kept.
(b) A torus-winding four-loop ($0,2,7,5$) closes only through the

$0$, wrapping in $z$. Its raised and lowered charge are offset by half a
cell, $\delta_z=-\tfrac12$, so it is removed. Because the transported
charge is half a lattice vector, the flux phase is $e^{-i\bvarphi/2}$
and must be swept \emph{twice} (period $4\pi$) to dephase it -- the naive
$2\pi$ leaves a residual.}
\label{fig:transport}
\end{figure}

\paragraph{The average.}
The method is to average the \emph{Hamiltonian} over the flux,
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\;\;
\overline{\Heff}=\int \Heff(\bvarphi)\,\frac{d\bvarphi}{(\text{period})^3},
\qquad
c_{\mathcal C}\to c_{\mathcal C}\,\overline{e^{-i\bvarphi\cdot(\bL^{-1}\bdelta_{\mathcal C})}} .
\;\;}
\label{eq:avg}
\end{equation}
A process survives iff its transport phase averages to unity, i.e.\ iff
$\bL^{-1}\bdelta_{\mathcal C}\in\Z^3$, i.e.\ iff $\bdelta_{\mathcal C}\in
\bL\Z^3$. Two points, elaborated and proved in
\cref{sec:why,sec:comp}, are essential and are where the naive procedure
fails:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] \emph{Translational invariance.} The phase must sit on every
bond. In the boundary-only gauge a winding loop has a virtual path built
from interior bonds alone, which carries no phase and survives the
average -- leaving half the coupling. The uniform gauge closes this
loophole.
\item[(ii)] \emph{Extended period.} A winding ring exchange transports
emergent charge by \emph{half} a lattice vector ($\bL^{-1}\brho=\tfrac12$;
verified for every winding four-loop and wrapping hexagon on both
production clusters), so its transport phase
$e^{-i\bvarphi\cdot(\bL^{-1}\bdelta)}=e^{-i\bvarphi/2}$ winds at half the
rate of $\bvarphi$ and is $\bvarphi$-periodic only modulo $4\pi$.
Averaging over the naive $[0,2\pi)^3$ therefore does \emph{not} project
(the phase makes half a turn, leaving a $\sim\!40\%$ residual); the
doubled period $[0,4\pi)^3$ is required. (Equivalently, the flux quantum
for the half-integer emergent charge is twice the $2\pi$ boundary-twist
quantum.)
\end{itemize}
The average is at the \emph{operator} level: one averages $\Heff$, then
diagonalizes. Averaging an \emph{observable} (a specific $C(T)$ or
$S(\bq,\omega)$) instead fails in any gauge (\cref{sec:why}).

%=====================================================================
\section{Why it works}
\label{sec:why}

\subsection{Ring exchanges are sums over virtual paths}

The naive expectation is that a winding ring exchange picks up its
holonomy $e^{-i\bw\cdot\bvarphi}$ and averages to zero. This is false in
an instructive way.

\begin{theorem}[Matched-pair factorization]
\label{thm:matched}
Let $\mathcal C$ be an ice-preserving cycle of length $2m$ and winding
$\bw$. At the leading order at which $O_{\mathcal C}$ appears, its
coefficient in the boundary-gauge twisted $\Heff(\bvarphi)$ is
\begin{equation}
c_{\mathcal C}(\bvarphi)=-\frac{g_{\mathcal C}}{M}\sum_{\mu=1}^{M}
e^{-i\bN_\mu\cdot\bvarphi},\qquad \sum_\mu\bN_\mu=\bw\ (M{=}2),
\end{equation}
summed over the $M\ge2$ perfect matchings of $\mathcal C$ into disjoint
NN dimer moves, $\bN_\mu$ the wrap of matching $\mu$. The
gauge-invariant magnitude is
$|c_{\mathcal C}(\bvarphi)|=g_{\mathcal C}|\cos(\tfrac12\bw\cdot\bvarphi)|$
for a two-matching loop.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
At leading order $O_{\mathcal C}$ is generated only by flipping each of
its $2m$ spins once, i.e.\ by a product of $m$ disjoint NN dimer moves --
a perfect matching; an even cycle has $M\ge2$. Each hop $S^+_iS^-_j$
carries $e^{-i\bn_{ij}\cdot\bvarphi}$, so matching $\mu$ carries
$e^{-i\bN_\mu\cdot\bvarphi}$. For a $4$-cycle the two matchings traverse
each bond once, $\bN_1+\bN_2=\bw$, giving
$|1+e^{-i\bw\cdot\bvarphi}|=2|\cos(\tfrac12\bw\cdot\bvarphi)|$.
\end{proof}

An operator thus lives in several transport sectors at once; the
holonomy is only the \emph{relative} phase of its matchings, and the
magnitude \emph{vanishes} at $\bw\cdot\bvarphi=\pi$ -- which a single
homology phase never does. In the boundary gauge a winding loop's
zero-wrap matching ($\bN_\mu=\bm0$) is phase-free and survives any
average, leaving $\tfrac12g_{\mathcal C}$. The smooth gauge removes this
loophole because it weights a process by its transport
$\bdelta$ [\cref{eq:phase}], and \emph{both} matchings of a winding loop
transport charge.

\subsection{The smooth-gauge average removes winding, keeps contractible}

\begin{theorem}[Transport selection]
\label{thm:transport}
Let $\ell_0=\min_{\bw\neq\bm0}|\bw\cdot\bL|$. If every ring exchange has
dipole $|\brho_{\mathcal C}|<\ell_0$, then the extended-period smooth-gauge
average \eqref{eq:avg} deletes every loop with $\brho_{\mathcal C}\neq\bm0$
at all orders in $\Jpm$ and keeps every loop with $\brho_{\mathcal C}=\bm0$
at full strength; on the production clusters these are exactly the winding
and the contractible loops.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The average keeps $O_{\mathcal C}$ iff $\bdelta_{\mathcal C}\in\bL\Z^3$;
since $\bN\!\cdot\!\bL\in\bL\Z^3$, this is
$\brho_{\mathcal C}\in\bL\Z^3$, independent of order. With
$|\brho_{\mathcal C}|<\ell_0$ the only lattice vector it can equal is
$\bm0$. A genuine bulk plaquette (regular $[111]$-hexagon or its
four-tetrahedron analogue) is centrosymmetric, so $\brho=\bm0$ and it is
kept. A winding loop has $\brho\neq\bm0$: otherwise
$\bdelta_1+\bdelta_2=\bw\!\cdot\!\bL$ with $|\bw\!\cdot\!\bL|\ge\ell_0$
would force some $|\bdelta_\mu|\ge\tfrac12\ell_0$ while $\bdelta_\mu=
\brho+\bN_\mu\bL$ with $\brho=\bm0$ makes $\bdelta_\mu$ a lattice
vector, hence $\bm0$ or $\ge\ell_0$ -- contradiction. So winding loops
are deleted.
\end{proof}

On the production clusters the hypothesis holds with room to spare: the
dipole of \emph{every} four-loop and wrapping hexagon has magnitude
$|\brho|=a/\sqrt2$, below $\ell_0=a$ (cubic) and $\sqrt2\,a$ (FCC), while
every contractible hexagon has $\brho=\bm0$ identically -- the
$0/0/1$ pattern of \cref{tab:census}. The one honest bias: a
\emph{contractible} process with a nonzero dipole (possible only for
non-centrosymmetric loops at order $\Jpm^4$ and beyond) would also be
removed; within the NN model these are $O(\Jpm^4/\Jzz^3)$, below $\ghex$.

\subsection{Why the naive versions fail}
\begin{corollary}
\label{cor:fail}
\emph{(a) Boundary gauge:} the operator average keeps $\tfrac12
g_{\mathcal C}$ (the zero-wrap matching is phase-free).
\emph{(b) Naive period:} the smooth-gauge average over $[0,2\pi)^3$
leaves a residual, because $\bL^{-1}\brho=\tfrac12$ is half-integer; the
doubled period $[0,4\pi)^3$ is required.
\emph{(c) Observable average:} averaging $\mathcal O(g)$ rather than
$\Heff$ keeps every even power of the gauge-invariant
$|c_{\mathcal C}|=g_{\mathcal C}|\cos(\tfrac12\bw\cdot\bvarphi)|$, so the
spurious scale survives in any gauge.
\end{corollary}

\section{Computational verification}
\label{sec:comp}

Each claim is checked exactly on the $16$-site cluster.

\paragraph{(1) Matched-pair magnitude.}
For a winding four-loop [sites $(0,2,8,10)$, $\bw=(-1,0,-1)$] the exact
second-order coefficient obeys, along $\bw$,
$|c(\bvarphi)|/\gfour=1.000,0.866,0.707,0.500,0.000,\dots
=|\cos(\tfrac12\bw\cdot\bvarphi)|$ to all digits, vanishing at
$\bw\cdot\bvarphi=\pi$.

\paragraph{(2) The average, gauge and period.}
The winding four-loop coefficient under the operator-level average is
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lcc}
\toprule
average & coeff.\ $/\gfour$ & $\Tpk/\ghex$ \\
\midrule
none (bare) & $-1.00$ & $3.18$ \\
boundary gauge & $-0.50$ & $1.63$ \\
smooth gauge, $[0,2\pi)^3$ & $+0.40$ & $0.84$ \\
smooth gauge, $[0,4\pi)^3$ (extended) & $0.00$ & $0.62$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
while a contractible hexagon is $+\ghex$ in every case (survival
$0/0/1$, \cref{tab:census}, to machine precision). Only the uniform
gauge over the extended period removes the loop; both naive choices leave
a residual.

\paragraph{(3) Two-peak specific heat.}
\Cref{fig:ct} shows the full $C(T)$ from exact $16$-site diagonalization
($12870$ states in $S^z_{\rm tot}=0$), with the gauge band cleaned by
the translationally-invariant average and the spinon sector taken from
the same spectrum. The low-$T$ gauge (photon) peak moves from the
artifact scale $\gfour$ (bare) to the photon scale $\ghex$ under the
average, while the high-$T$ charge (spinon) peak at $T\sim\Jzz/3$ is
essentially unchanged -- as it must be, being uncontaminated physics
outside the ice manifold. Averaging the observable instead barely moves
the gauge peak (\cref{cor:fail}(c)).

\section{Demonstration and outlook}
\label{sec:demo}

The gauge peak of the exact $32$-site FCC $\Heff$ (full spectrum of the
$2970$-state ice manifold), in units of $\ghex$: bare periodic ED sits
at the artifact scale and drifts up as $\gfour/\ghex=\Jzz/3|\Jpm|$ toward
weak coupling ($5.4,3.1$ at $\Jpm=-0.03,-0.05$), while the
translationally-invariant average brings it to $O(1)$ ($0.6,0.3$). The
same holds for the emergent-photon structure factor
$S^{zz}(\bq,\omega)=\sum_n|\langle n|S^z_\bq|0\rangle|^2\delta(\omega
-\omega_n)$, whose low-$\omega$ weight moves from $\sim\gfour$ (bare) to
$\sim\ghex$ (averaged) on both clusters and both flux signs.

The couplings scale as $\Jpm^k$ exactly, so one effective-Hamiltonian
build serves every coupling and both signs. The program is:
\emph{(A)} benchmark the averaged $0$-flux $C(T)$, $S(T)$ and photon
$S^{zz}$ against sign-problem-free QMC at a common coupling;
\emph{(B)} cross into $\pi$-flux and compute the same quantities where
QMC cannot; \emph{(C)} evaluate at the measured exchange parameters of
Ce$_2$Zr$_2$O$_7$, Ce$_2$Sn$_2$O$_7$, Ce$_2$Hf$_2$O$_7$ and confront the
neutron $S(\bq,\omega)$ and the specific heat. The uncontaminated spinon
continuum is supplied by full-Hilbert-space finite-temperature Lanczos,
with twist averaging there used for its standard purpose --
Brillouin-zone interpolation of the genuine bands.
