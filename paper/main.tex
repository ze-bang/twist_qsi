\title{Winding-free exact diagonalization of quantum spin ice:
benchmarks and convergence criteria}

\author{Z.-B. Zhou}
\affiliation{(affiliation)}

\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
Exact diagonalization of quantum spin ice on a periodic cluster can generate
noncontractible four-spin tunneling at second order, parametrically before
the physical six-spin ring exchange.  We formulate a nonperturbative
winding-free remedy:
exactly diagonalize a transported-dipole source family, canonically pull its
isolated ice-descended band to a common basis, and project its zero Fourier
component.  At $(\Jpm,\Jpp)/\Jzz=(0.046,0)$, the character sequence converges from
$M=3$ to $M=4$ within $0.50\%$ in centered operator norm, with minimum ice
overlap $0.762$.  The winding-free band moves the cubic-16 heat-capacity peak from
$0.0255\Jzz$ to $0.00146\Jzz$, compared with the quantum Monte Carlo value
$0.00110\Jzz$, while retaining the exact microscopic complement.  The
longitudinal response simultaneously moves from $0.08065\Jzz$ to a near-zero
$\Gamma$ line and $0.00535\Jzz$ at the three allowed $X$ points.  Thus the
method is nonperturbatively converged on cubic-16, but the cluster is not yet
a quantitative surrogate for bulk quantum spin ice.
\end{abstract}

\emph{Motivation.}---Quantum spin ice (QSI) realizes a compact $U(1)$ gauge
theory in a pyrochlore magnet: the two-in--two-out constraint is Gauss's law,
defects are electric charges, and collective hexagon tunneling generates the
emergent photon~\cite{Hermele2004,Gingras2014,Benton2012}.  This low scale is
experimentally consequential.  In particular, the two heat-capacity features
reported in Ce$_2$Hf$_2$O$_7$ motivate calculations that retain both the
spin-ice crossover and quantum dynamics~\cite{Smith2025}.  Numerically, the
problem is divided by sign.  Quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) reaches the zero-flux
XXZ model and resolves its thermodynamics and dynamics~\cite{Shannon2012,Huang2018},
whereas the opposite sign relevant to $\pi$-flux candidates has a sign
problem.  Exact diagonalization (ED) has no sign problem, but its periodic
clusters alter the shortest allowed gauge processes.

We address that finite-size problem without replacing the microscopic model
by an effective Hamiltonian.  Perturbation theory is used only to identify
the offending path and test its topology; production results come from exact
microscopic eigenspaces.  The controlled numerical limit is the
transport-character resolution $M\to\infty$.  On the sign-free side,
winding removal must also improve convergence toward QMC.  Exact removal of a known
artifact is not by itself proof of convergence to the bulk.

\emph{The finite torus changes the leading process.}---We study
\begin{equation}
 \begin{aligned}
 H={}&\Jzz\sum_{\langle ij\rangle}S_i^zS_j^z
 -\Jpm\sum_{\langle ij\rangle}(S_i^+S_j^-+S_i^-S_j^+)\\
 &+\Jpp\sum_{\langle ij\rangle}(S_i^+S_j^++S_i^-S_j^-),
 \end{aligned}
 \quad \Jzz>0 .
\label{eq:model}
\end{equation}
For an $S^z$ basis state $|s\rangle$, let
$Q_t(s)=\sum_{i\in t}S_i^z(s)$.  The ice-rule projector is simply
\begin{equation}
 \Pice=\sum_{\substack{s:\,Q_t(s)=0\\\text{for every }t}}
 |s\rangle\langle s| .
\label{eq:ice_projector}
\end{equation}
Thus $\Pice$ is the orthogonal projector onto the subspace satisfying the ice
rule on every tetrahedron.  In the infinite pyrochlore lattice the shortest
contractible process acting within this subspace is a hexagon.  The protocol
below includes both transverse couplings; the analytic scales quoted next and
all present benchmark curves use the QMC-matched slice $\Jpp=0$.
Its third-order matrix element has scale
$g_6=12|\Jpm|^3/\Jzz^2$~\cite{Hermele2004}.  On a torus, however, a path may
close through a periodic image.  Cubic-16 contains 36 winding four-cycles in
two winding classes [Figs.~\ref{fig:geometry}i.b) and
\ref{fig:geometry}i.c)] and 16 contractible hexagons
[Fig.~\ref{fig:geometry}i.a)].  The four-spin
process enters at second order with $g_4=4\Jpm^2/\Jzz$, so
$g_4/g_6=\Jzz/(3|\Jpm|)$ diverges in the nominally controlled limit.  FCC-32
reduces but does not eliminate the problem: it has 24 winding four-cycles,
32 contractible hexagons, and 96 wrapping hexagons.

The distinction is visible before assigning any perturbative amplitude.
Lift each exchange sequence $\gamma$ to the periodic covering lattice and add
its signed spin-transfer displacements.  The two four-loops transport a finite
dipole $\bq_\gamma\ne0$ across the torus, although they return to the same
finite-cell spin configuration.  By contrast, the alternating transfers on a
contractible hexagon sum to $\bq_\gamma=0$.  Our protocol projects the exact
ice-connected band onto this winding-free ($\bq=0$) character, and therefore removes
the former while retaining the latter.

\emph{Winding-free construction.}---For an oriented bond, let
$\bm d_{ij}=\bm r_j-\bm r_i-\bm n_{ij}^{T}L$ be its displacement and
$\bm c_{ij}=\bm r_i+\bm r_j-\bm n_{ij}^{T}L$ its pair-flip coordinate in the
periodic covering lattice.  We diagonalize the explicitly sourced microscopic
Hamiltonian
\begin{align}
 H(\btheta)=H_0-\Jpm\sum_{\langle ij\rangle}
 \left(e^{2i\btheta\cdot\bm d_{ij}}S_i^+S_j^-
 +e^{-2i\btheta\cdot\bm d_{ij}}S_i^-S_j^+\right)\nonumber\\
 {}+\Jpp\sum_{\langle ij\rangle}
 \left(e^{-2i\btheta\cdot\bm c_{ij}}S_i^+S_j^+
 +e^{2i\btheta\cdot\bm c_{ij}}S_i^-S_j^-\right).
\label{eq:sourced}
\end{align}
Equation~(\ref{eq:sourced}) is the full microscopic finite-cluster
Hamiltonian, not an effective Hamiltonian.  No expansion or virtual-state
denominator enters its definition.
For a sequence $\gamma$ of microscopic moves, each step $\ell$ acts on the
stored oriented bond $(i_\ell,j_\ell)$.  Its lifted polarization increment is
\begin{equation}
 \Delta\bm p_\ell=\begin{cases}
 -\bm d_{ij},&T_\ell=S_i^+S_j^-,\\
 +\bm d_{ij},&T_\ell=S_i^-S_j^+,\\
 +\bm c_{ij},&T_\ell=S_i^+S_j^+,\\
 -\bm c_{ij},&T_\ell=S_i^-S_j^-.
 \end{cases}
\label{eq:polarization_increment}
\end{equation}
Multiplication of the source phases gives $e^{-i\btheta\cdot\bq_\gamma}$ with
$\bq_\gamma=2\sum_{\ell\in\gamma}\Delta\bm p_\ell$.
Completed ice-to-ice paths have integer $\bq_\gamma$: contractible paths have
$\bq_\gamma=0$, whereas the targeted paths with nonzero net winding transport
have $\bq_\gamma\ne0$.  This phase-product statement is an exact identity
between microscopic matrix elements.

The symbol $P_{\btheta}$ denotes the exact spectral projector of
$H(\btheta)$ onto the isolated band continuously connected to
the ice-rule subspace as the transverse couplings are turned on,
\begin{equation}
 P_{\btheta}=\frac{1}{2\pi i}\oint_{\Gamma_{\btheta}}
 (z-H(\btheta))^{-1}dz,\qquad
 \operatorname{rank}P_{\btheta}=\operatorname{rank}\Pice.
\label{eq:spectral}
\end{equation}
It is distinct from the fixed ice projector.  The polar map
\begin{equation}
 W_{\btheta}=P_{\btheta}\Pice
 (\Pice P_{\btheta}\Pice)^{-1/2}
\label{eq:polar}
\end{equation}
canonically identifies the exact band with the fixed ice-rule reference
subspace.  The
pulled-back operator is
$h(\btheta)=W_{\btheta}^{\dagger}H(\btheta)W_{\btheta}$.  We define
\begin{equation}
 h_M=\frac{1}{M^3}
 \sum_{\bm m\in\mathbb Z_M^3}
 h(2\pi\bm m/M).
\label{eq:character}
\end{equation}
Equation~(\ref{eq:character}) retains $\bq=0\pmod M$ and converges to the
continuous zero-character integral as $M\to\infty$.  A closing band gap or a
singular $\Pice P_{\btheta}\Pice$ is an explicit failure of the method.  The
construction is nonperturbative on the finite cluster because
$H(\btheta)$, $P_{\btheta}$, and the inverse square root in
Eq.~(\ref{eq:polar}) are evaluated at the physical couplings, without a
$\Jpm/\Jzz$ or $\Jpp/\Jzz$ expansion.  A formal expansion would recover all
mixed orders in the two transverse couplings; the production
calculation instead evaluates this function directly and projects its zero
Fourier coefficient.  Low-order Schrieffer--Wolff rows~\cite{Bravyi2011}
only audit the loop interpretation.  For thermal observables we define the
hybrid replacement spectrum
\begin{equation}
 Z_{\rm wf}=Z_{\rm full}-Z_{\rm periodic\ band}+Z_{\rm wf\ band},
\label{eq:replacement}
\end{equation}
and apply the same identity to the first two energy moments.  It leaves the
microscopic complement intact, but it is a diagnostic spectrum rather than
the spectrum of the original $H(0)$.  Derivations and numerical details are
given in the Supplemental Material~\cite{supp}.

\emph{Internal tests.}---Direct row enumeration through third order gives a
binary topology test.  On both clusters, Eq.~(\ref{eq:character}) removes
100\% of winding four-loop weight and wrapping-hexagon weight and retains
100\% of contractible-hexagon weight.  The row classification and retained
weight are defined explicitly in the Supplemental Material~\cite{supp}.  This
is an order-two/order-three audit of the transport label; the exact-band
projection itself is not truncated at either order.  At
$(\Jpm,\Jpp)/\Jzz=(0.046,0)$,
cubic-16 moves
from the periodic $T_{\rm p}/\Jzz=0.02554$ to the winding-free value
$0.001252$.  FCC-32 gives
$0.008770$ and $0.000608$ respectively within the same third-order diagnostic.
These FCC-32 values are not production thermodynamics.

The exact cubic-16 calculation uses one source convention at every $M$.
The zero-source band has minimum ice overlap 0.762 and fixed-$S^z$ gap
$0.627\Jzz$.  All $M=3,4$ source points have larger overlaps and gaps, with
maximum Ritz residual $3.7\times10^{-12}$.  The centered operator changes by
6.78\% from $M=2$ to 3, but only 0.498\% from $M=3$ to 4, passing the 5\%
character gate.  The low-order audit shows that $M=2$ already removes the
shortest winding four-loops while retaining every contractible hexagon
[the retained-weight table in the Supplemental Material~\cite{supp}].  It is
not an all-order zero-transport projector:
every nonzero $\bq=2\bm k$ is still aliased with zero.  The sizable $M=2$ to 3
change and subsequent $M=3$ to 4 agreement therefore test higher-order
characters, not additional removal of the audited shortest loops.

\emph{Winding-free dynamics.}---We hold the physical probe fixed while
changing the band Hamiltonian.  Let $W_0$ be the exact zero-source isometry in
Eq.~(\ref{eq:polar}) and define
\begin{equation}
 \widetilde S_a^z(\bq)=W_0^\dagger
 \left(\sum_{j\in a}e^{-2\pi i\bq\cdot\bm r_j}S_j^z\right)W_0 .
\label{eq:dssf_source}
\end{equation}
For either $h(\bm0)$ or $h_{M=4}$, we compute
\begin{align}
 S^{zz}(\bq,\omega)&=\frac{1}{Nd_0}
 \sum_{g\in\mathcal G}\sum_{n\notin\mathcal G}\sum_{a=0}^3
 |\langle n|\widetilde S_a^z(\bq)|g\rangle|^2 \nonumber\\[-2pt]
 &\hspace{2.5cm}\times G_\eta[\omega-(E_n-E_g)],
\label{eq:dssf}
\end{align}
where $\mathcal G$ is the ground multiplet and
$\eta=6\times10^{-4}\Jzz$.  Cubic-16 has only four translation momenta,
$\Gamma$ and three symmetry-related $X$ points, which reduce to the two
classes shown below.

The zero-source canonical isometry reproduces the cached periodic operator to
$2.7\times10^{-14}$ relative error.  The periodic ground state is unique;
the winding-free ground multiplet has dimension three.  The $X$-point
centroid moves from $0.08069\Jzz$ to $0.005372\Jzz$, demonstrating that the
construction reorganizes both energies and matrix elements rather than merely
shifting a thermodynamic peak.

\emph{External QMC gate.}---Huang \emph{et al.} simulate precisely
Eq.~(\ref{eq:model}) at $(\Jpm,\Jpp)/\Jzz=(0.046,0)$ using continuous-time worm QMC and
report thermodynamics together with QMC-SAC dynamics on $8^3$ primitive
cells~\cite{Huang2018}.  A vector extraction of their curves gives low- and
high-temperature peaks at $T/\Jzz=0.001100$ and $0.2105$.  The converged
$M=4$ result places the low peak at $0.001463\Jzz$ and reduces the
low-temperature heat-capacity RMSE from 0.0859 to 0.02327, a 72.9\%
improvement [Fig.~\ref{fig:validation}ii)].  It nevertheless misses the
preregistered 0.02 tolerance.
Entropy also fails: its RMSE is 0.04509, again above 0.02
[Fig.~\ref{fig:validation}ii)].
The same paper provides the next dynamics gate, $S^{zz}$ and $S^{+-}$ at
$T/\Jzz=0.001$, 0.04, and 0.1; matching its momentum points, normalization,
and SAC resolution remains to be done.

\emph{Outlook.}---The exact $M=3,4$ agreement establishes winding removal
beyond low-order perturbation theory, but cubic-16 remains outside the QMC
heat and entropy tolerances.  The remaining campaign is a character-converged
FCC-32 ABC/XYZ calculation followed by a Ce$_2$Hf$_2$O$_7$ heat-capacity fit;
entropy and matched neutron channels are holdout tests, not fit normalizations.
