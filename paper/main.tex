\title{Exact diagonalization of the quantum spin ice gauge sector beyond
quantum Monte Carlo:\\ removing torus-winding contamination by
zero-transport projection}

\author{}
\date{\today}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
The thermodynamics of quantum spin ice (QSI) is governed by an emergent
compact U(1) gauge field. Its unfrustrated \emph{$0$-flux} sector
($\Jpm>0$) is accessible to sign-problem-free quantum Monte Carlo (QMC),
but the frustrated \emph{$\pi$-flux} sector ($\Jpm<0$) realized in the
dipolar--octupolar cerium pyrochlores carries a QMC sign problem and has
no controlled finite-temperature benchmark. Small-cluster exact
diagonalization (ED) is the natural alternative -- but on the smallest
periodic clusters it is
contaminated by ice-preserving four-site cycles that close only by
winding the cluster torus. They enter the effective Hamiltonian at
\emph{second} order, $\gfour=4\Jpm^2/\Jzz$, parametrically above the
genuine photon coupling $\ghex=12|\Jpm|^3/\Jzz^2$. It is tempting to
remove them by averaging the ED over U(1) boundary twists
$\bvarphi$. We show that this fails for two independent reasons, both
confirmed by exact numerics, and we construct the projector that
succeeds. (i)~\emph{Twist phases attach to virtual paths, not to
loops.} A ring exchange over a cycle $\mathcal C$ is generated by the
\emph{two perfect matchings} of $\mathcal C$ into nearest-neighbour
dimer moves; its coupling at twist $\bvarphi$ is
$c_{\mathcal C}(\bvarphi)=-\tfrac{\gfour}{2}\,
[e^{-i\bN_A\cdot\bvarphi}+e^{-i\bN_B\cdot\bvarphi}]$ with
$\bN_A+\bN_B=\bw$ the loop winding. On both production clusters
[$1{\times}1{\times}1$ cubic (16 sites) and $2{\times}2{\times}2$ FCC
(32 sites)] \emph{every} winding four-loop and every wrapping hexagon
has one matching carrying zero net wrap ($\bN_A=\bm 0$, generically two
bulk-bond dimer moves), which no boundary twist can dephase: in the
boundary-bond gauge even a perfect operator-level twist average retains
exactly \emph{half} of every spurious coupling. (In the
translationally-invariant gauge the operator average instead removes it
entirely -- there it coincides with the zero-transport projector below;
the two failure modes of naive twist averaging are the boundary gauge
and, gauge-invariantly, the averaging of observables rather than the
Hamiltonian.) A gauge-invariant
corollary, $|c_{\mathcal C}|\propto|1+e^{-i\bw\cdot\bvarphi}|$, means
individual couplings switch \emph{off} (not sign-flip) at twist corners
with $\bw\cdot\bm p$ odd -- verified level-by-level against exact corner
spectra. (ii)~\emph{Averaging observables is weaker still}: the free
energy is nonlinear in $\Heff$, so corner-averaging $C(T,\bvarphi)$
cancels only terms odd in the spurious couplings; even powers survive at
full strength. Exactly on the 16-site cluster at $\Jpm=-0.1\Jzz$, the
low-$T$ peak moves only from $0.037\Jzz$ (bare) to $0.031\Jzz$
(corner-averaged observable) and to $0.018\Jzz$ (corner-averaged
\emph{operator}, obtained by exact L\"owdin downfolding at each corner
-- the half-coupling scale $\gfour/2=0.02\Jzz$), never approaching the
photon scale. The projector that works keeps only processes with zero
\emph{net charge transport} $\bdelta=\bm 0$ -- the gauge-invariant label
that a uniform emergent vector potential couples to. It kills every
winding four-loop and wrapping hexagon exactly, at all orders, while
preserving every contractible hexagon at full strength (verified to
machine precision on both clusters), and it moves the 16-site peak to
$0.0075\Jzz$, at the hexagon scale $\ghex=0.012\Jzz$, an order of
magnitude below the bare artifact. We give exact corrected corner
spectra (the ground state at $\bvarphi=\bm 0$ is a singlet; previously
quoted ``7-fold ground manifolds'' were Lanczos artifacts), the
combinatorial censuses, and practical recipes: path-selected effective
Hamiltonians in perturbation theory, and a nonperturbative dipole mask
on the exactly downfolded Hamiltonian beyond it. Because the same
selection rule cleans not only the Hamiltonian but every ice-manifold
operator, the projected cluster becomes a quantitative laboratory for
the emergent gauge sector: we outline how it delivers benchmarked
$\pi$-flux gauge thermodynamics -- validated against $0$-flux QMC and
extended to the sign-problem regime QMC cannot reach -- with the
flux-locked thermodynamic response of the emergent photon as the target
observable.
\end{abstract}

\section{The finite-size short-loop problem}
\label{sec:problem}

The XXZ pyrochlore Hamiltonian in local axes is
\begin{equation}
H_{\mathrm{XXZ}} = \Jzz \sum_{\langle ij\rangle} S^z_i S^z_j
- \Jpm \sum_{\langle ij\rangle} \bigl(S^+_i S^-_j + S^-_i S^+_j\bigr),
\qquad \Jpm = -\tfrac{1}{4}(J_{xx}+J_{yy}),
\label{eq:HXXZ}
\end{equation}
where $\langle ij\rangle$ runs over nearest-neighbour (NN) bonds. For
$|\Jpm|/\Jzz\ll 1$, third-order perturbation theory on the $\twoinout$
(ice-rule) manifold reduces \cref{eq:HXXZ} to the
Hermele--Fisher--Balents ring-exchange
Hamiltonian~\cite{HermeleFisherBalents2004,Savary2017}
\begin{equation}
\Heff^{(\mathrm{HFB})} = -\frac{12\,\Jpm^3}{\Jzz^2}\sum_{\mathrm{hex}}
\bigl(S^+ S^- S^+ S^- S^+ S^-\bigr)_{\mathrm{hex}} + \text{h.c.},
\label{eq:HFB}
\end{equation}
summed over hexagonal plaquettes; $\ghex \equiv 12|\Jpm|^3/\Jzz^2$
sets the emergent-photon scale. The sign of the coefficient is locked to
$\mathrm{sgn}(\Jpm^3)$: unfrustrated ($0$-flux) for $\Jpm>0$, frustrated
($\pi$-flux) for $\Jpm<0$.

On a finite torus the periodic identifications generate additional short
ice-preserving cycles that wrap the cluster. The dominant ones are
four-site cycles threading four tetrahedra; they arise already at
\emph{second} order,
\begin{equation}
\Heff^{(\mathrm{spur})}\;=\;-\,\gfour\!\!\sum_{\substack{\mathcal{C}\;\text{4-cycle}\\ \bw_{\mathcal{C}}\neq\bm{0}}}
\bigl(S^+_a S^-_b S^+_c S^-_d\bigr)_{\mathcal{C}} + \text{h.c.},
\qquad \gfour=\frac{4\Jpm^2}{\Jzz},
\label{eq:Hspur}
\end{equation}
with $\gfour$ \emph{sign-blind} in $\Jpm$ (second order) and
\begin{equation}
\frac{\gfour}{\ghex}=\frac{\Jzz}{3|\Jpm|}
=\begin{cases} 3.3 & (\Jpm=-0.1\,\Jzz)\\ 6.7 & (\Jpm=+0.05\,\Jzz),\end{cases}
\label{eq:g4_over_ghex}
\end{equation}
so the finite-size artifact is parametrically \emph{larger} than the
physics one wants to extract. \Cref{sec:combinatorics} enumerates these
cycles exactly: $36$ on the $1{\times}1{\times}1$ cubic (16-site)
cluster and $24$ on the $2{\times}2{\times}2$ FCC (32-site) cluster,
every one non-contractible ($\bw\neq\bm 0$); there are \emph{no}
contractible ice-preserving 4-cycles. \Cref{fig:loops} contrasts a
genuine bulk hexagon with a winding four-cycle.

Exactly at $\Jpm=-0.1\,\Jzz$, $\bvarphi=\bm 0$, the low spectrum of the
16-site cluster is (energies above the ground state, exact sparse ED in
the $S^z_{\rm tot}=0$ sector)
\begin{equation}
E_n = \{0;\; 0.10603\;(\times 4);\; 0.12595\;(\times 9);\;\dots\},
\label{eq:barespec}
\end{equation}
a \emph{singlet} ground state with gap $\Delta=0.10603\,\Jzz$ set by the
$\gfour$ scale, and the specific heat carries a spurious low-$T$
Schottky peak at $T\simeq 0.037\,\Jzz$, on the four-loop scale
$\gfour=0.04\,\Jzz$ rather than the photon scale
$\ghex=0.012\,\Jzz$.\footnote{An earlier version of this manuscript
quoted a ``$7$-fold quasi-degenerate ground manifold'' here, and
degeneracies between $5$ and $10$ across twist corners. Exact
re-diagonalization shows these were artifacts of degeneracy detection on
Lanczos spectra: the correct ground-state degeneracies are $1$ at
$\bvarphi=\bm 0$ and at the three single-$\pi$ corners, and $2$ at the
four remaining corners (\cref{tab:corners}). The quoted \emph{gaps} were
correct.}

\section{Twist phases attach to virtual paths, not to loops}
\label{sec:twist-theory}

\subsection{Twist setup}

The cluster is a flat 3-torus with first homology $\mathbb{Z}^3$. A U(1)
twist $\bvarphi\in[0,2\pi)^3$ is inserted, in the standard
\emph{boundary-bond (minimum-image) gauge}, by attaching a phase to
every NN bond that crosses the periodic boundary, with integer wrap
vector $\bn_{ij}=\mathrm{round}[(\br_j-\br_i)/L]$:
\begin{equation}
H_\perp(\bvarphi) = -\Jpm \sum_{\langle ij\rangle}
\bigl(S^+_i S^-_j\,e^{-i\,\bn_{ij}\cdot\bvarphi}
+S^-_i S^+_j\,e^{+i\,\bn_{ij}\cdot\bvarphi}\bigr).
\label{eq:Hperp-twist}
\end{equation}
The Ising part is $\bvarphi$-independent, $S^z_{\rm tot}$ is conserved
at every $\bvarphi$, and $\bvarphi=\bm 0$ recovers \cref{eq:HXXZ}.

\subsection{The matched-pair factorisation}
\label{sec:matching}

It is tempting to argue -- as an earlier version of this manuscript did
-- that every effective ring exchange over a closed loop $\mathcal{L}$
acquires the Wilson phase $e^{-i\bw_{\mathcal L}\cdot\bvarphi}$ of its
homology class, so that a twist average projects onto $\bw=\bm 0$
exactly. \emph{This is false}, for an elementary reason: the phase of a
perturbative process is the product of the phases of the \emph{bond
terms the process actually uses}, and the XXZ ring exchange never
traverses the loop as a connected walk. The operator
$O_{\mathcal C}=S^+_aS^-_bS^+_cS^-_d$ is a product of two commuting
dimer moves, and there are exactly \emph{two perfect matchings} of the
cycle into NN dimer moves that generate it: $\{ab,\,cd\}$ (matching $A$)
and $\{bc,\,da\}$ (matching $B$), each with two time orderings and equal
energy denominators. Reinstating the twist phases per bond,
\begin{equation}
c_{\mathcal C}(\bvarphi)\;=\;-\,\frac{\gfour}{2}\Bigl[
e^{-i\bN_A\cdot\bvarphi}+e^{-i\bN_B\cdot\bvarphi}\Bigr],
\qquad
\bN_A=\bn_{ab}+\bn_{cd},\quad \bN_B=\bn_{bc}+\bn_{da},
\label{eq:matching}
\end{equation}
with
\begin{equation}
\bN_A+\bN_B=\bw_{\mathcal C}.
\label{eq:NANB}
\end{equation}
Only the \emph{sum} \cref{eq:NANB} is fixed by homology; the individual
matching windings $\bN_{A,B}$ are properties of the virtual paths (and
of the chosen gauge). The same structure holds for the hexagon at third
order (three dimer moves, two matchings) and, suitably generalised, at
every order. \Cref{fig:matchings} illustrates the two matchings of a
winding four-loop.

Two exact corollaries follow.

\paragraph{(a) Corner rule: couplings switch off, they do not flip sign.}
The magnitude
\begin{equation}
|c_{\mathcal C}(\bvarphi)|=\frac{\gfour}{2}\,\bigl|1+e^{-i\bw\cdot\bvarphi}\bigr|
=\gfour\left|\cos\frac{\bw\cdot\bvarphi}{2}\right|
\label{eq:cornerrule}
\end{equation}
is gauge invariant. At a twist corner $\bvarphi=\pi\bm p$,
$\bm p\in\{0,1\}^3$, a winding loop's coupling is therefore
$-\gfour$ if $\bw\cdot\bm p$ is even and \emph{exactly zero} if
$\bw\cdot\bm p$ is odd. Corners do not build a $\pm$ interference
pattern over loops; they \emph{deactivate} subsets of loops. This is
confirmed level-by-level by the exact corner spectra of
\cref{tab:corners}: the number of active four-loops falls from $36$
(bare) to $20$ ($|\bm p|_\pi=1$) to $12$ ($|\bm p|_\pi\geq 2$), and the
low gap falls with it.

\paragraph{(b) The bulk matching survives every twist average.}
On both production clusters, \emph{every} winding four-loop and every
wrapping hexagon has one matching with $\bN=\bm 0$: its dimer moves
carry zero net wrap (generically, both moves lie on bulk bonds, as in
\cref{fig:matchings}), so its phase is $\bvarphi$-independent.\footnote{Verified to machine precision by
a path-resolved numerical Schrieffer--Wolff computation that tracks
$\bN$ for every virtual path: on the $1{\times}1{\times}1$ cubic and
$2{\times}2{\times}2$ FCC clusters, exactly $1/2$ of the coefficient
magnitude of every winding four-loop and every wrapping hexagon carries
$\bN=\bm 0$, and \emph{all} of the coefficient of every contractible
hexagon carries $\bN=\bm 0$ (\cref{tab:census-matching}).} That matching
is untouched by any boundary twist. Consequently the twist average of
the effective \emph{Hamiltonian} -- over the eight corners, over any
grid, or over the continuum -- retains
\begin{equation}
\overline{\Heff^{(\rm spur)}}\;=\;-\,\frac{\gfour}{2}
\sum_{\mathcal C}\bigl(O_{\mathcal C}+O^\dagger_{\mathcal C}\bigr)
\;+\;\ldots,
\label{eq:halfsurvival}
\end{equation}
half of every spurious coupling, at every order at which a bulk matching
exists. Twist averaging in the boundary-bond gauge \emph{cannot} remove
the short-loop contamination, even in principle, even at the operator
level. The good news is equally sharp: every contractible hexagon has
\emph{both} matchings on bulk bonds ($\bN_A=\bN_B=\bm 0$), so the
genuine ring exchange passes through all of this untouched, at full
strength, at every twist.

\subsection{Why the loop-holonomy argument fails: a self-correction}

The superficially plausible identity
$\Heff^{(n)}(\bvarphi)=\sum_{\bw}e^{i\bw\cdot\bvarphi}\Heff^{(n)}{}_{\!,\bw}(0)$
with $\bw$ the \emph{homology} winding of the flipped-site loop -- the
basis of the earlier version of this work, and of the intuition
``averaging projects onto contractible loops'' -- would hold if the
alternating $S^\pm$ factors around a loop always picked up the loop
holonomy. They do not: the decomposition \cref{eq:matching} shows the
operator receives contributions in \emph{two} different twist-Fourier
sectors $\bN_A$ and $\bN_B$, and only their sum is homological. The
holonomy $e^{-i\bw\cdot\bvarphi}$ appears exactly once, as the
\emph{relative} phase of the two virtual paths -- whence the
gauge-invariant corner rule \cref{eq:cornerrule} -- but the
\emph{absolute} Fourier content is path-dependent, and the bulk
matching sits at $\bN=\bm 0$ regardless of $\bw$.

\section{Averaging observables is weaker still}
\label{sec:obs-vs-op}

Even if one ignores the half-survival and averages, there is a second,
independent error mode: in practice one averages \emph{observables}
[e.g.\ $\overline{C}(T)=\tfrac18\sum_{\bvarphi}C(T,\bvarphi)$], not the
Hamiltonian. The projection identities of \cref{sec:matching} are
statements about objects \emph{linear} in $\Heff$. The free energy is
not linear, so its twist-Fourier components mix all winding sectors
nonlinearly: corner-averaging an observable cancels only the terms
\emph{odd} in each spurious coupling, while all even powers survive at
full strength.

A two-level caricature makes this sharp. Let the ice-manifold physics
consist of one genuine scale $\varepsilon\sim\ghex$ and one spurious
coupling that follows the corner rule, $g(\bvarphi) =
\gfour\cos^2(\bw\cdot\bvarphi/2)$: the gap is
$\Delta(\bvarphi)=2\sqrt{\varepsilon^2+g(\bvarphi)^2}$. Averaging
$\Delta$ (or any observable built from it) over corners leaves a mixture
of $2\sqrt{\varepsilon^2+\gfour^2}$ and $2\varepsilon$ -- the
$\gfour^2$ term \emph{never cancels} -- whereas the averaged
\emph{Hamiltonian} would carry $g=\gfour/2$ (\cref{eq:halfsurvival}),
and only the transport projector of \cref{sec:transport} reaches
$2\varepsilon$. Since $\gfour/\ghex\gg1$ [\cref{eq:g4_over_ghex}], the
surviving even-power contamination \emph{dominates} the photon scale.

The exact 16-site numbers land precisely on this hierarchy
(\cref{fig:avg16,tab:schemes}): at $\Jpm=-0.1\,\Jzz$ the low-$T$ peak
sits at $0.0366\,\Jzz$ (bare), $0.0313\,\Jzz$ (corner-averaged
observable: barely moves), $0.0178\,\Jzz$ (corner-averaged
\emph{operator}, i.e.\ the exact L\"owdin-downfolded
$\Heff(\bvarphi)$ averaged as a matrix over the eight corners: the
half-coupling scale $\gfour/2=0.02\,\Jzz$ of \cref{eq:halfsurvival}),
and only the transport projector reaches $0.0075\,\Jzz$, at the photon
scale. The corner-mean gap, $0.0391\,\Jzz$, is numerically
indistinguishable from $\gfour=0.04\,\Jzz$ -- the observable average
never left the artifact scale.

\section{The zero-transport projector}
\label{sec:transport}

\subsection{The gauge-invariant selection rule}

What labels a virtual process gauge-invariantly is not a winding class
but its \emph{net charge transport}
\begin{equation}
\bdelta \;=\; \sum_{\text{hops}} \bigl(\text{displacement of the moved
boson}\bigr)
\;=\; \underbrace{\sum_{i\,\text{raised}}\!\br_i-\!\sum_{j\,\text{lowered}}\!\br_j}_{\textstyle \bm\rho\;(\text{operator dipole})}
\;+\; \bN\!\cdot\!\bm L,
\label{eq:delta}
\end{equation}
the physical displacement vector of the $S^z$ (boson) density moved by
the process; $\bdelta$ is what a \emph{uniform} vector potential couples
to. In the smooth gauge -- twist spread over every bond in proportion
to its real displacement, $\theta_{ij}=\bm A\cdot\bm d_{ij}$ with
$\bm L_k\cdot\bm A=\varphi_k$ -- each process carries the phase
$e^{-i\bm A\cdot\bdelta}$. Averaging the effective Hamiltonian over the
\emph{extended} twist period (the smooth-gauge Hamiltonian returns to
itself, up to a diagonal gauge, only after $\varphi_k\to\varphi_k+8\pi$
on the quarter-integer pyrochlore coordinates) is then a projector onto
$\bdelta=\bm 0$:
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\;\;\overline{\Heff}^{\,\bdelta}
=\sum_{\text{processes}:\;\bdelta=\bm 0} (\text{that process}).\;\;}
\label{eq:deltaproj}
\end{equation}

The selection rule \cref{eq:deltaproj} does exactly what the naive
homology projector was supposed to do, with a geometric argument that
holds at \emph{all orders}:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
\item \textbf{Winding four-loops die.} A four-loop matching transports
two bosons by two NN dimer displacements, $|\bdelta_M|\le
2d_{\rm NN}=a/\sqrt2\approx 0.71a$, while
$\bdelta_A+\bdelta_B=-\bw\!\cdot\!\bm L$ with $|\bw\cdot\bm L|\ge a$
(cubic) or $\sqrt2 a$ (FCC $2{\times}2{\times}2$): neither matching can
have $\bdelta=\bm 0$. Higher-order paths generating the same operator
can only shift $\bdelta$ by full cluster translations, and the operator
dipole $\bm\rho$ is a quarter-integer vector too short to reach another
translation: winding four-loops carry $\bdelta\neq\bm 0$ in \emph{every}
realisation, to all orders.
\item \textbf{Contractible hexagons survive at full strength.} The
$[111]$ pyrochlore hexagons are regular, so the alternating displacement
sum of either matching vanishes identically: both virtual paths have
$\bdelta=\bm 0$ and pass the projector untouched.
\item \textbf{Wrapping hexagons die} by the same length-count
($3d_{\rm NN}\approx1.06a$ cannot reach $|\bw\cdot\bm L|\geq\sqrt2 a$ on
either cluster).
\end{itemize}
The census of \cref{tab:census-matching} (last column) confirms all
three statements to machine precision at orders $\Jpm^2$ and $\Jpm^3$ on
both production clusters: survival fractions exactly $0$, $1$, $0$.

One honest bias remains at higher orders: a \emph{contractible} loop
process with a nonzero transport dipole ($\bm\rho\neq\bm 0$, possible
for non-centrosymmetric loops at order $\Jpm^4$ and beyond) is also
removed. Within the strict NN XXZ model the leading such objects are
$O(\Jpm^4/\Jzz^3)$ corrections, parametrically below $\ghex$; we flag
them rather than hide them.

\subsection{Implementations}
\label{sec:implementations}

\paragraph{(i) Path-selected perturbation theory.} A numerical
Schrieffer--Wolff expansion in the ice manifold that tracks
$(\bN,\bm\rho)$ per virtual path implements \cref{eq:deltaproj} as a row
filter; we carry it to fourth order including the
$-\tfrac12\{PVG^2VP,\,PVGVP\}$ correction. Cost: seconds (16 sites) to
minutes (32 sites).

\paragraph{(ii) Exact downfolding plus dipole mask (recommended).}
Beyond perturbation theory the projector has a remarkably simple
nonperturbative implementation. Diagonalize $H$ \emph{once} at
$\bvarphi=\bm0$, L\"owdin-project the lowest ice band onto the
ice-configuration basis (the des Cloizeaux effective Hamiltonian;
overlap singular values $\geq0.58$ on the 16-site cluster, so the band
is cleanly ice-derived), and \emph{zero out every matrix element whose
flipped-site set carries a fractional dipole}: element
$(c,c')$ is kept iff
$\bm\rho=\sum_{i\,\rm raised}\br_i-\sum_{j\,\rm lowered}\br_j
\equiv\bm0 \pmod{\bm L}$.
By \cref{eq:delta} this keeps exactly the $\bdelta=\bm 0$ operator
content, up to wrap-around ($|\bN|\geq1$) contributions to
$\bm\rho$-trivial elements, which are of higher perturbative order.
No twist grid is needed at all. Validation against the path-selected
construction (i): low-$T$ peak $0.00122$ vs $0.00127\,\Jzz$ at
$\Jpm=-0.05$, and $0.00647$ vs $0.00747\,\Jzz$ at $-0.1$ -- where the
mask value is the more reliable of the two, being nonperturbative
(the difference is consistent with the $\sim15\%$ fourth-order
truncation of (i) at this coupling). On the 16-site cluster the mask
retains $16\%$ of the ice-basis matrix elements: the diagonal plus the
contractible-hexagon connectivity.

\paragraph{(ii$'$) Smooth-gauge averaging (conceptual form).} The same
projector is realised as an average of downfolded matrices over a
smooth-gauge twist grid spanning the extended period
$\varphi_k\to\varphi_k+8\pi$; the corner-averaged-operator curve of
\cref{fig:avg16} is this construction on the $\{0,\pi\}^3$ subgrid and
realises \cref{eq:halfsurvival} exactly. In practice the dipole mask
(ii) supersedes it.

\paragraph{(iii) Counterterm cross-check.} Since the winding loops are
enumerable, one can add $+\gfour\sum_{\mathcal C} (O_{\mathcal C}+h.c.)
+ |\gamma_6|\sum_{\rm wrap\,hex}(\ldots)$ to the \emph{bare} Hamiltonian
and diagonalize exactly. This cancels the artifact only at leading
order -- precisely the objection raised against counterterms before --
but it needs no downfolding and provides an independent exact-machinery
reference; residual disagreement with (i)/(ii) measures the
higher-order winding content.

\subsection{16 sites: the hierarchy of schemes}

\Cref{fig:avg16}(a) and \cref{tab:schemes} give the central result,
already anticipated in \cref{sec:obs-vs-op}: observable averaging
$0.037\to0.031\,\Jzz$ (a $15\%$ move; the corner-mean gap remains at
$\gfour$), operator averaging $\to 0.018\,\Jzz$ ($=\gfour/2$ scale,
as \cref{eq:halfsurvival} demands), transport projector $\to
0.0075\,\Jzz$, below $\ghex=0.012\,\Jzz$ and consistent with the
finite-cluster hexagon-only model (the 16 contractible hexagons on 90
ice states). The counterterm reference lands at $0.019\,\Jzz$ at this
coupling, and at $0.0025\,\Jzz$ versus the projector's $0.0013\,\Jzz$ at
$\Jpm=-0.05$: the excess is the $O(\Jpm^4)$ winding content that a
leading-order counterterm cannot cancel. Since that residue scales as
$\Jpm^4$ while $\ghex\propto|\Jpm|^3$, its \emph{relative} size grows
as $\Jpm\to0$ -- counterterms are structurally unable to expose the
photon scale, sharpening the objection raised against them in the
earlier version of this work.

\subsection{32 sites: why the corner-averaged low-$T$ structure looks
pathological}

On the $2{\times}2{\times}2$ FCC cluster the corner rule deactivates
winding loops corner-by-corner just as on 16 sites, but with only $24$
loops (three times fewer per site) the surviving frustrated subsets
produce wildly dispersed corner gaps: at $\Jpm=-0.05$ the eight corners
have low gaps $\{5.2\times10^{-3};\;8.9\times10^{-5}\ (\times4);\;
1.55\times10^{-2}\ (\times3)\}\,\Jzz$ -- \emph{more than two decades} of
spread, with four corners nearly gapless. A corner-averaged
$\overline C(T)$ is then a superposition of Schottky bumps at every one
of these scales: its resolvable maximum stays at the artifact scale
[$0.0056\,\Jzz$ vs the projected photon peak at $0.0005\,\Jzz$,
\cref{tab:schemes}], its height collapses (the weight is split among
incommensurate bumps), and a sizable fraction of the low-$T$ weight is
pushed an order of magnitude \emph{below} the photon scale, to
$T\sim0.4\times9\times10^{-5}\,\Jzz$ -- beneath the temperature floor of
typical FTLM grids ($T_{\rm min}=5\times10^{-3}$ in our production
runs). In practice the ``low peak'' of a corner-averaged FCC run is
therefore anomalously low and smeared, and none of its structure sits at
the physical scale. The operator average again stalls at half coupling,
and the transport projector lands at the hexagon scale, $0.0005\,\Jzz
\approx 0.3\,\ghex$, close to the bulk Schottky position $0.42\,\ghex$
[\cref{fig:avg16}(b), \cref{tab:schemes}]. At larger couplings the
same mechanism appears in a milder guise that matches production
full-ED experience: bare and corner-averaged peak \emph{positions}
nearly coincide while the averaged peak is \emph{lower} -- the
twist-invariant hexagons pin the position, and the height loss is the
winding sector reacting to flux insertion
(see \cref{sec:necessity} for why this is itself a contamination
measurement).

\section{The projected cluster as a laboratory: gauge-sector responses}
\label{sec:responses}

The transport selection rule \cref{eq:deltaproj} is not a property of
the Hamiltonian alone: it classifies \emph{every} operator acting in
the ice sector. Any induced response operator -- for instance the
second-order action $\delta H$ of a uniform transverse source
$-\lambda\sum_i(S^+_i+S^-_i)$, whose spinon-pair virtual processes are
worked out in the companion source-response notes -- decomposes into a
$\bdelta=\bm0$ part (bulk gauge physics) and a winding part (finite-size
artifact), and the \emph{same} dipole mask projects it. This upgrades
the projector from a spectral fix to a definition: \emph{the bulk gauge
sector of a small torus is the zero-net-transport sector of all its
ice-manifold operators}, Hamiltonian and responses alike.

It also supplies the sharpest validation test we have. The measured
source response of the bare cluster,
$\Tpk(\lambda)=\Tpk(0)(1-\kappa\lambda^2)$ with slope
$B=\kappa|\Jpm|\Jzz$, is known (companion notes) to be a
near-cancellation of a bulk term (the flux-resolved spinon-vacuum
polarizability, a rescaling of the gauge spectrum) against a
winding-four-loop counter-term. If the projector removes exactly the
winding sector, the projected cluster's $B$ must jump to the
polarizability-only value. It does:
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lccc}
\toprule
$\Jpm=-0.05\,\Jzz$ & bare cluster & projected cluster & polarizability-only \\
\midrule
$B$ & $+3.28$ & $+9.55$ & $+9.90$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
(the bare value matches the production full-ED measurement $+3.20$; the
projected value is obtained by applying the dipole mask of
implementation (ii) to \emph{both} the downfolded Hamiltonian and the
exactly computed response operator). Two conclusions. First, the
projector removes the winding sector coherently across observables --
spectrum and response together -- to within a few percent. Second, and
of independent physical interest, the projected cluster delivers a
genuinely \emph{bulk} gauge-sector quantity that the bare cluster
mangles by a factor of three: the flux-resolved vacuum polarizability
of the emergent U(1) (the response is $2$--$3\times$ stronger, with
opposite sign, in the $0$-flux sector). Photon-scale thermodynamics
was the projector's first deliverable; bulk response functions are its
second.

\section{Combinatorics of the short loops}
\label{sec:combinatorics}

This section is unchanged in substance from the earlier version -- the
loop censuses were correct -- and is retained for completeness, with the
matching-resolved census of \cref{tab:census-matching} superseding the
winding-only table as the operationally relevant one.

A simple cycle is ice-preserving iff every tetrahedron is touched by
$0$ or $2$ of its sites. On the pyrochlore NN graph an ice-preserving
4-cycle is the boundary of a ``square'' of four distinct tetrahedra;
such a square does not close in $\mathbb{R}^3$ (the girth of the
ice-preserving loop set is six), so \emph{every} ice-preserving 4-cycle
wraps: $36$ on $(1,1,1)$ cubic [$24$ single-axis, e.g.\ $\bw=(1,0,0)$,
and $12$ two-axis, e.g.\ $\bw=(1,1,0)$] and $24$ on $(2,2,2)$ FCC
[$12{:}12$]. The FCC cluster carries three times fewer spurious
4-cycles per site ($0.75$ vs $2.25$) because its primitive translation
$|\bm a_k|=a/\sqrt2$ spans two NN spacings. Hexagons: $64$
ice-preserving $[111]$-planar 6-cycles on the cubic cluster, of which
$16$ are contractible (bulk density $\nu_c=1$) and $48$ wrap; $128$ on
FCC, $32$ contractible ($\nu_c=1$), $96$ wrapping. The coupling of every
4-cycle is exactly $-4\Jpm^2/\Jzz$ [two matchings $\times$ two orderings
$\times$ denominator $\Jzz$: a single NN hop creates two $3{:}1$
tetrahedra at $\Jzz/2$ each], and of every hexagon exactly
$-12\Jpm^3/\Jzz^2$, both reproduced to machine precision by the
numerical Schrieffer--Wolff coefficients.

\section{Cluster geometry: the bulk hexagon coupling is not renormalised}
\label{sec:geometry}

The geometric audit of the earlier version stands: on every faithful
cluster the per-site density of contractible hexagons is the bulk value
$\nu_c=1$ (\cref{fig:cluster_geometry}), and the transport projector
preserves exactly these hexagons at full strength
(\cref{tab:census-matching}). The residual finite-size error of the
projected problem is therefore purely kinematic: the photon disperses
linearly and the smallest nonzero cluster wavevector bounds the lowest
photon energy, $\omega_{\rm min}\sim c\,k_{\rm min}$, so the projected
Schottky peak sits above the bulk position $0.42\,\ghex$ until
$L\,N_\varphi$ is large. The 16-site projected peak at
$0.0075\,\Jzz\approx0.62\,\ghex$ against the bulk $0.005\,\Jzz$ is
consistent with this picture. Note that the ``twist grids refine the
Brillouin zone'' bonus applies to the \emph{observable} average in its
free-particle sense and must not be conflated with artifact removal:
the two goals now belong to two different constructions.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

Twist boundary conditions in QSI ED do less than advertised and more
than expected, in a way this work makes precise.

\emph{Less than advertised:} the classic picture -- ring exchanges carry
their homology holonomy, so twist averaging projects onto contractible
loops -- fails because ring exchanges are generated by \emph{pairs of
virtual paths}, and one path of every winding loop carries zero net
wrap. Averaging the Hamiltonian over boundary twists removes at
most half of each spurious coupling [\cref{eq:halfsurvival}]; averaging
observables, as done in practice, removes only interference terms and
leaves the artifact scale essentially intact
(\cref{sec:obs-vs-op}). Both failure modes are confirmed exactly on the
16-site cluster, and they explain two long-standing puzzles of
production runs: the twist-averaged 16-site peak that never approaches
$\ghex$, and the anomalously low, smeared low-$T$ structure of
corner-averaged 32-site FCC runs (near-gapless frustrated corners push
spectral weight far below the photon scale and below typical FTLM
temperature floors).

\emph{More than expected:} the corner rule \cref{eq:cornerrule} says a
single well-chosen twist switches off entire winding classes exactly
[e.g.\ $\bvarphi=(\pi,\pi,\pi)$ kills all $24$ single-axis four-loops on
the 16-site cluster], and the corner-resolved spectra become a clean
spectroscopy of the winding sector (\cref{tab:corners}). The
flux-dependence of that sector is physical: the four-loop channel is
sign-blind in $\Jpm$ while the hexagon channel flips sign, so the two
cooperate at $0$-flux and compete at $\pi$-flux. This is directly
measurable: in the companion transverse-source study, the measured
source curvature of the gauge peak on the bare cluster contains a
winding-four-loop component that counteracts the dominant
spinon-continuum response; the four-loop component (of order $\mp7$ to
$\pm14$ in the slope $B$, opposite on the two flux sides) is precisely
the contamination that transport projection removes, turning the
projected $\kappa$ into a clean probe of the surviving spinon response.

The correct scheme is the zero-transport projector
\cref{eq:deltaproj}: gauge-invariant, exact at all orders for the
winding loops, transparent about its one bias (transport-dipole-carrying
contractible processes at $O(\Jpm^4)$), and implementable either
perturbatively (path selection) or nonperturbatively (exact downfolding
plus the dipole mask). On the 16-site cluster it
moves the low-$T$ peak from the artifact scale $0.037\,\Jzz$ to the
photon scale $0.0075\,\Jzz$ at $\Jpm=-0.1\,\Jzz$ -- a factor of five --
with the remaining distance to the bulk $0.42\,\ghex$ accounted for by
Brillouin-zone resolution, not by contamination.

\subsection{When is the projection actually necessary? The 32-site PBC
benchmark}
\label{sec:necessity}

An empirical challenge must be met head-on: full finite-$T$ ED on the
$2{\times}2{\times}2$ FCC cluster at $\Jpm\simeq-0.1\,\Jzz$, with plain
periodic boundaries, already places the low-$T$ peak in good agreement
with the $\ghex=12|\Jpm|^3/\Jzz^2$ scale; corner-averaged and PBC
results coincide in position there, the averaged peak merely being
lower (slower entropy release). If the largest tractable cluster gets
the photon scale right without any machinery, what is the machinery
for? Four answers, in increasing order of importance.

\emph{First, that benchmark sits at the most benign point of the whole
parameter space, and the framework explains why.} Three suppressions
coincide at (FCC, $\pi$-flux, $\Jpm=-0.1$): the FCC cluster carries
three times fewer winding loops per site than the cubic one
(\cref{sec:combinatorics}); at $\pi$-flux the winding channel is
additionally frustrated against the hexagons
(\cref{tab:census-matching}, companion analysis); and the contamination
ratio $\gfour/\ghex=\Jzz/(3|\Jpm|)$ is \emph{smallest} at the largest
coupling. Agreement there confirms the census logic; it does not test
the regime where the artifact lives.

\emph{Second, the necessity is a statement about the coupling
range, and about scaling rather than one point.} The artifact grows
parametrically toward small $|\Jpm|$ -- exactly where the QSI regime is
robust and where one wants numerics. On the same FCC-32 cluster whose
$-0.1$ point looks fine, the ratio of the bare peak to $\ghex$ grows
from $\sim1.5$ ($-0.1$) to $3.1$ ($-0.05$) to $5.4$ ($-0.03$)
[\cref{tab:schemes}], i.e.\ bare/projected reaches an order of
magnitude, while at $-0.03$ four of the eight twist corners become
essentially gapless ($4\times10^{-6}\,\Jzz$). Equivalently: genuine
photon thermodynamics must collapse as $|\Jpm|^3$; a
$\gfour$-contaminated peak drifts toward $|\Jpm|^2$ scaling (the ratios
above track $\gfour/\ghex=\Jzz/3|\Jpm|$), so agreement at a single
coupling can be -- and at $-0.1$ partly is -- a crossing-point
coincidence. The projector is what buys the \emph{whole} coupling
range and the scaling test.

\emph{Third, the observed twist-sensitivity is itself the measured
contamination.} The genuine gauge sector is provably twist-invariant
-- every contractible hexagon carries zero twist phase at every
$\bvarphi$ (\cref{sec:matching}) -- so if the $-0.1$ FCC physics were
purely photonic, corner averaging would change \emph{nothing}. The
reported suppression of the averaged peak (``slower entropy release'')
is the frustrated winding sector reacting to flux insertion: the
corner rule switches loop subsets off, the corner gaps disperse
(two decades at $-0.05$, \cref{sec:demo}), and the averaged
entropy release smears. In other words, the very observation quoted
against the machinery is a measurement of the residual artifact --
twist response is the \emph{diagnostic}, transport projection the
\emph{cure}.

\emph{Fourth, peak position is the least sensitive observable.} Even
where the bare peak position looks right, gauge-sector \emph{responses}
remain wrong by $O(1)$ factors: the source-response slope of the bare
cluster is a near-cancellation ($B=+3.3$ vs the bulk-relevant $+9.9$ at
$\Jpm=-0.05$; \cref{sec:responses}), i.e.\ off by a factor of three at
couplings where $C(T)$ looks converged. And beyond $32$ sites full
finite-$T$ ED ends, while the projector's perturbative implementation
(path selection in the ice manifold) scales to the $48$- and $64$-site
clusters whose Brillouin zones begin to resolve the photon
(\cref{fig:cluster_geometry}) -- PBC luck at $32$ sites offers no such
path.

\subsection{Objective: a benchmarked laboratory for the \texorpdfstring{$\pi$}{pi}-flux gauge sector}
\label{sec:objective}

The projector's ultimate purpose is not a cleaner specific-heat peak but
access to physics that is otherwise out of reach. Three facts combine.
(i)~The effective ring-exchange (gauge) Hamiltonian lives in the
$2970$-state ice manifold of the $2{\times}2{\times}2$ FCC cluster, so
its \emph{full} spectrum -- and hence numerically exact gauge-sector
thermodynamics, $C(T)$ and $S(T)$ down to $T\to0$ -- is obtained by
dense diagonalization, without the low-$T$ ceiling of stochastic
finite-$T$ methods. (ii)~The transport projector removes the winding
contamination that otherwise corrupts this spectrum, and does so for
\emph{every} ice-manifold operator, not only $H$
(\cref{sec:responses}). (iii)~The QSI transverse coupling is
sign-problem-free for QMC only at $\Jpm>0$; the $\pi$-flux sector
($\Jpm<0$) of the cerium pyrochlores is a QMC dead zone.

This defines a concrete program, and the natural culmination of the
present method. Benchmark the projected $0$-flux gauge thermodynamics
against published sign-problem-free QMC at the same coupling; then cross
into $\pi$-flux, where the target observable is the flux-locked response
of the emergent photon to a uniform conjugate field -- a
background-free thermodynamic discriminant of the gauge flux (softening
at $\pi$-flux, hardening at $0$-flux), computable from the same
transport-projected ice-manifold operators and measurable by
dilatometry or magnetocalorimetry. The couplings scale as $\Jpm^k$
exactly, so a single effective-Hamiltonian build serves the entire sweep
and both flux signs; the campaign, resource estimates, and go/no-go
gates are detailed in the accompanying simulation plan.
