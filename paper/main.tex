\title{Exact diagonalization of the $\pi$-flux quantum spin ice gauge sector
beyond quantum Monte Carlo: removing torus-winding contamination by
zero-transport projection}

\author{Z.-B.~Zhou}
\affiliation{(affiliation)}

\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
The $\pi$-flux regime of quantum spin ice (QSI)---the regime relevant to the
dipolar--octupolar cerium pyrochlores, and in particular to
Ce$_2$Hf$_2$O$_7$---carries a fermion sign problem that blocks unbiased
quantum Monte Carlo (QMC).  Exact diagonalization (ED) is sign-problem free,
but on the small periodic clusters it requires it suffers a specific,
previously underappreciated pathology: the torus supports ice-preserving
ring-exchange loops that close only by winding around the boundary.  The
shortest of these is a four-spin loop generated at second order in the
transverse coupling, $\gfour=4\Jpm^2/\Jzz$, whereas the physical
emergent-photon ring exchange is the contractible six-spin hexagon generated
at third order, $\ghex=12|\Jpm|^3/\Jzz^2$.  Their ratio
$\gfour/\ghex=\Jzz/3|\Jpm|$ diverges in the perturbative regime, so the
artifact dominates precisely where the QSI description is cleanest, displacing
the low-temperature specific-heat peak and the low-frequency non-spin-flip
structure factor by nearly an order of magnitude.  We show that ordinary
boundary-twist averaging removes only half of every winding coupling, and
introduce a single gauge-invariant diagnostic, the transported dipole
$\bdelta=\brho+\bN\bL$, whose vanishing selects exactly the contractible
processes.  Projecting the ice-manifold effective Hamiltonian onto
$\bdelta=0$ yields an artifact-free cluster whose low-energy thermodynamics
and $\Szz(\bq,\omega)$ we compute in the QMC-inaccessible $\pi$-flux sector,
benchmarked against sign-free $0$-flux QMC.  This turns a small periodic
cluster into a controlled laboratory for the artificial photon of
Ce$_2$Hf$_2$O$_7$.
\end{abstract}

\paragraph{Introduction.}---Quantum spin ice is the leading candidate for a
three-dimensional, experimentally accessible $U(1)$ quantum spin
liquid~\cite{Hermele2004,GingrasMcClarty2014,Savary2016}.  Its low-energy
theory is compact quantum electrodynamics: the two-in--two-out ``ice''
constraint on the pyrochlore lattice plays the role of a Gauss law, gapped
``spinon'' defects are the emergent electric charges, and the collective
tunnelling of spins around hexagonal plaquettes builds a gapless emergent
photon~\cite{Hermele2004,Benton2012,Shannon2012}.  The sign of the plaquette
(``ring-exchange'') amplitude distinguishes two gauge sectors: a $0$-flux
sector and a frustrated $\pi$-flux sector~\cite{LeeOnodaBalents2012}.  In the
dipolar--octupolar (DO) cerium pyrochlores
Ce$_2$Zr$_2$O$_7$~\cite{Gaudet2019,Gao2019}, Ce$_2$Sn$_2$O$_7$~\cite{Sibille2020},
and Ce$_2$Hf$_2$O$_7$~\cite{Poree2023}, the octupolar exchange places the
material in or near the $\pi$-flux regime, making a quantitative,
material-specific theory of this sector timely.

The methodological obstacle is sharp.  Sign-problem-free QMC exists only for
the $0$-flux sign of the transverse coupling ($\Jpm>0$); the $\pi$-flux sector
($\Jpm<0$), the one realized in the cerium magnets, has a sign
problem~\cite{Shannon2012,Kato2015}.  ED does not, but it is confined to small
clusters.  Here we identify and cure a finite-size pathology that is specific
to the QSI \emph{gauge sector} and that has, we argue, distorted low-energy ED
and finite-temperature Lanczos benchmarks in the $\pi$-flux regime.

\paragraph{The model.}---We take the nearest-neighbor XXZ model in the local
$[111]$ frames,
\begin{equation}
H=\Jzz\!\sum_{\langle ij\rangle}\!S_i^zS_j^z
 -\Jpm\!\sum_{\langle ij\rangle}\!\left(S_i^+S_j^-+S_i^-S_j^+\right),
 \quad |\Jpm|\ll\Jzz,
\label{eq:model}
\end{equation}
with $\Jzz>0$.  The Ising part is minimized by the extensively degenerate ice
manifold $\ice$ (every tetrahedron two-in--two-out); a single tetrahedron
carries energy $\tfrac{\Jzz}{2}Q_t^2$ with charge $Q_t=\sum_{i\in t}S_i^z$, so
a spinon pair costs $\Jzz$.  Degenerate perturbation theory in $\Jpm$ inside
$\ice$ gives the ring-exchange gauge Hamiltonian order by order,
$\Heff=PVRVP+PVRVRVP+\cdots$, with $P$ the projector onto $\ice$ and
$R=-QH_0^{-1}Q$ the non-ice resolvent.

\paragraph{Physical vs.\ artifact loops.}---Because the pyrochlore lattice is
the line graph of the bipartite diamond lattice, the shortest \emph{contractible}
ice-preserving ring exchange is a six-site hexagon, generated at third order
with amplitude
\begin{equation}
\ghex=\frac{12|\Jpm|^3}{\Jzz^2},
\label{eq:ghex}
\end{equation}
the amplitude of the emergent photon.  A periodic cluster, however, is the
flat torus $T^3=\mathbb R^3/\bL\mathbb Z^3$.  On the torus a path can close by
running through the identified boundary; we label such a closed path by its
winding $\bw=\sum_{(ij)}\bm n_{ij}$, where $\bm n_{ij}$ is the periodic image
of bond $(ij)$.  A four-site ice-preserving loop cannot close in real space
(no diamond four-cycle exists), so on the standard clusters every such loop is
a winding loop, $\bw\neq0$.  It is generated already at second order with
amplitude
\begin{equation}
\gfour=\frac{4\Jpm^2}{\Jzz},\qquad
\frac{\gfour}{\ghex}=\frac{\Jzz}{3|\Jpm|}\;\xrightarrow[\Jpm\to0]{}\;\infty .
\label{eq:g4}
\end{equation}
The finite-size artifact is thus \emph{parametrically larger} than the physics
it contaminates, and most severe in the controlled perturbative regime
[Fig.~\ref{fig:concept}(a)].  A from-scratch enumeration confirms zero
contractible four-loops and, e.g., $16$ ($32$) contractible hexagons on the
$16$-site cubic ($32$-site FCC) cluster.

\paragraph{Why twist averaging is insufficient.}---A ring exchange is not a
single virtual path but a sum over perfect matchings and time orderings.
Attaching boundary-twist phases $e^{-i\bN_r\cdot\bphi}$ to each path and
averaging over the eight corners $\phi_a\in\{0,\pi\}$ projects onto rows with
even accumulated image $\bN_r$.  On the target clusters every winding four-loop
possesses a matching with $\bN=0$ (built from interior bonds, its
ice-closure relying on a wrapping \emph{tetrahedron} that the boundary twist
does not see).  Consequently exactly half of each spurious coupling survives
any boundary-gauge twist average [Fig.~\ref{fig:concept}(b)]; averaging a
nonlinear observable such as $C(T)$ afterwards is weaker still, removing only
odd powers.

\paragraph{The transported dipole.}---The correct, gauge-invariant object is
the net displacement transported by the effective operator.  For a row
connecting ice states $s\to t$, let $\brho_{t,s}=\sum_{i\in R}\br_i-\sum_{j\in
L}\br_j$ be the home-cell dipole of the raised ($R$) minus lowered ($L$)
spins, and $\bN_r$ the accumulated image of the virtual path.  Define
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\;\bdelta_r=\brho_{t,s}+\bN_r\bL\;}
\label{eq:delta}
\end{equation}
$\bdelta_r$ is independent of the home cell and of the twist gauge.
Threading a \emph{uniform} vector potential $\bm A=\bphi\bL^{-1}$ on every
bond (not only boundary bonds) multiplies each row by $e^{-i\bm
A\cdot\bdelta_r}$; the operator-level average over the twist torus is the
Fourier projection onto $\bdelta_r=0$.  For the ring exchanges here
$\brho=\tfrac12\bw\bL$ exactly, so $\bdelta=0$ is equivalent to zero winding:
the projection keeps every contractible hexagon and removes every winding
four-loop and wrapping hexagon at all orders.  The artifact-free gauge
Hamiltonian is
\begin{equation}
\Hclean(\Jpm)=\sum_{r:\,\bdelta_r=0}c_r\,\Jpm^{k_r}\,|t_r\rangle\langle s_r| .
\label{eq:hclean}
\end{equation}
Crucially the projection acts on the operator before diagonalization; because
$C(T)$ and $\Szz$ are nonlinear in the spectrum, post-hoc averaging cannot
reproduce it.

\paragraph{Results.}---We build path-resolved Schrieffer--Wolff row tables at
$\Jpm=1$ (they scale as $\Jpm^k$ exactly, so a single build serves all
couplings and both signs), compute $\bdelta$ for every row, assemble both
$\Heff^{\rm bare}$ and $\Hclean$, and diagonalize densely on the ice manifold
($90$ states for the $16$-site cubic cluster, $2970$ for the $32$-site FCC
cluster).  Figure~\ref{fig:results} summarizes the effect.  Untreated, the
low-temperature specific-heat peak sits at $T_{\rm peak}\simeq\gfour\propto
\Jpm^2$ and drifts upward as $\Jzz/3|\Jpm|$ relative to the photon scale---the
smoking gun of the finite-size four-loop.  After projection the peak locks
onto $T_{\rm peak}=O(\ghex)\propto|\Jpm|^3$, independent of coupling in those
units, and the associated entropy plateau moves to the physical photon scale.
The projection is a pure removal of matrix elements: it cannot add spectral
weight, and the census verifies it deletes exactly the winding channels.

\paragraph{Connection to Ce$_2$Hf$_2$O$_7$.}---Among the DO cerium
pyrochlores, Ce$_2$Hf$_2$O$_7$ shows the cleanest low-temperature signatures
of a quantum-spin-ice ground state, with a broad low-$T$ specific-heat feature
and a gapless magnetic continuum consistent with an emergent
photon~\cite{Poree2023,Beare2023}.  The exchange lies in the octupolar
$\pi$-flux regime, where the sign problem forbids a direct QMC prediction of
these very observables.  Equation~\eqref{eq:hclean} supplies the missing
unbiased benchmark: at the material's $|\Jpm|/\Jzz$ the clean cluster predicts
(i) the temperature of the photon specific-heat feature at
$O(\ghex)=O(12|\Jpm|^3/\Jzz^2)$ rather than the spurious $\gfour$ scale, (ii)
the corresponding photon entropy release, and (iii) the low-frequency
non-spin-flip structure factor $\Szz(\bq,\omega)$ that inelastic neutron
scattering measures.  Because the artifact and the physics carry opposite
scaling in $|\Jpm|$, the projection is essential precisely at the small
$|\Jpm|/\Jzz\!\sim\!0.03$--$0.1$ inferred for these materials.  The
complementary high-energy spinon continuum ($\omega\sim\Jzz$) lives outside
the ice manifold, is uncontaminated by the winding four-loop, and is obtained
from full-Hilbert-space finite-temperature Lanczos.

\paragraph{Discussion.}---The transported dipole $\bdelta$ is a topological
diagnostic---half the homology winding of the ring-exchange loop---so the
$\bdelta=0$ projection is exact at every perturbative order and gauge
independent, unlike counterterm subtraction (whose residue grows as
$\Jpm\to0$) or twist averaging (which halves but never removes the artifact).
It converts the small $\pi$-flux cluster into a benchmarked laboratory for the
emergent photon of quantum spin ice, and delivers, in the sector where QMC
cannot, the finite-temperature and dynamical observables now being measured in
Ce$_2$Hf$_2$O$_7$.  A companion pedagogical note derives every step and every
number with an independent from-scratch code.
