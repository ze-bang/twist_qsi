\title{Winding-Free Exact Diagonalization of Quantum Spin Ice}

\author{Zhengbang Zhou}
\email{zhengbang.zhou@mail.utoronto.ca}
\affiliation{Department of Physics, University of Toronto, Toronto, Ontario M5S 1A7, Canada}

\begin{abstract}
Quantum spin ice realizes an emergent three-dimensional $U(1)$ gauge theory
whose low-energy dynamics is generated by six-spin ring exchange around
pyrochlore hexagons. The sign of this exchange selects zero- or $\pi$-flux
gauge backgrounds, yet the frustrated sign relevant to $\pi$-flux candidates
is inaccessible to sign-problem-free quantum Monte Carlo. Exact
diagonalization is sign unrestricted, but standard periodic clusters admit a
noncontractible four-spin process at second order, parametrically above the
physical third-order hexagon tunneling. We eliminate this finite-torus
artifact by coupling microscopic spin transfers to a source that records
their transport on the covering lattice and projecting the exact
ice-descended band onto its zero-transport Fourier component. On cubic-16,
the $M=3$ and $M=4$ projected operators agree within $0.013\%$ in centered
operator norm, and a third-order path classification removes every winding
four-loop while retaining every contractible hexagon. At
$(\Jpm,\Jpp)/\Jzz=(0.046,0)$, the low-temperature heat-capacity peak shifts
from $0.02554\Jzz$ in ordinary exact diagonalization to $0.001463\Jzz$, close
to the quantum Monte Carlo value $0.001100\Jzz$. The longitudinal $X$-point
excitation centroid simultaneously shifts from $0.08069\Jzz$ to
$0.005362\Jzz$. The residual thermodynamic discrepancies isolate finite-size
effects beyond the removed winding process.
\end{abstract}

\emph{Introduction.}---Quantum spin ice (QSI) is a $U(1)$ quantum spin liquid
with fractionalized excitations and an emergent gauge field whose gapless
photon mode provides a characteristic low-energy signature
\cite{Hermele2004,Gingras2014,Benton2012}. Recent experiments on
Ce$_2$(Zr,Sn,Hf)$_2$O$_7$ suggest that Ce-based pyrochlores may realize a
$\pi$-flux QSI, in which an emergent gauge flux of $\pi$ threads each
elementary hexagonal plaquette [Fig.~\ref{fig:summary}(i.a)]
\cite{Gao2019,Gaudet2019,Bhardwaj2022}. In Ce$_2$Zr$_2$O$_7$, no long-range
order or spin freezing is observed down to the lowest accessible
temperatures, whereas recent heat-capacity measurements on Ce$_2$Hf$_2$O$_7$
resolve two anomalies near $65\,$mK and $25\,$mK \cite{Smith2025}. Numerical
linked-cluster fits place the candidate parameters within the $\pi$-flux QSI
regime, motivating a microscopic explanation of this two-scale thermodynamic
structure.

The minimal microscopic setting for QSI is the nearest-neighbor
pseudospin-$1/2$ XYZ model
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
H_0={}&\Jzz\sum_{\langle ij\rangle}S_i^zS_j^z
-\Jpm\sum_{\langle ij\rangle}\left(S_i^+S_j^-+S_i^-S_j^+\right)\\
&+\Jpp\sum_{\langle ij\rangle}\left(S_i^+S_j^++S_i^-S_j^-\right),
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:model}
\end{equation}
with $\Jzz>0$. For $|\Jpm|,|\Jpp|\ll\Jzz$ the low-energy states obey the
spin-ice rule $Q_t\equiv\sum_{i\in t}S_i^z=0$ on every tetrahedron $t$; we
write $\Pice$ for the orthogonal projector onto that manifold, and call a
tetrahedron with $Q_t\ne0$ a spinon. Transverse
exchange first connects distinct ice configurations at third order, through
collective flips around elementary six-site hexagons, generating the
ring-exchange scale
\begin{equation}
g_6=\frac{12|\Jpm|^3}{\Jzz^2}.
\label{eq:g6}
\end{equation}
In the convention of Eq.~(\ref{eq:model}), $\Jpm>0$ gives the zero-flux QSI,
whereas $\Jpm<0$ gives the $\pi$-flux QSI. The parametrically small scale
$g_6$ is therefore a natural candidate for the lower-temperature
thermodynamic anomaly.

Obtaining a microscopic description of this regime using unbiased numerical
methods remains challenging. Quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) simulations access the
zero-flux QSI for $\Jpm>0$ \cite{Shannon2012,Kato2015,Huang2018} but
encounter a sign problem in the $\pi$-flux regime, $\Jpm<0$. Numerical
linked-cluster expansions fail to converge at the ultralow temperatures at
which the lower anomaly emerges. Finite-temperature tensor-network
calculations have reached a periodic 48-site three-dimensional Heisenberg
cluster, but only well above the ring-exchange scale \cite{Schaefer2020}.
Density-matrix renormalization group and minimally entangled typical thermal
states reach lower temperatures on pyrochlore tubes \cite{Hagymasi2021}, but
their quasi-one-dimensional geometry cannot support a gapless photon mode. A
sign-unrestricted microscopic treatment that resolves the three-dimensional
gauge dynamics at temperatures of order $g_6$ is therefore needed.

Exact diagonalization (ED) is exact on a finite cluster and imposes no sign
restriction, but is limited to small pyrochlore tori, notably the cubic
16-site and face-centered-cubic 32-site clusters. Their dominant artifact is
not size alone but the appearance of spurious four-site loops. On cubic-16, a
noncontractible four-spin path closes through the periodic boundary
[Figs.~\ref{fig:summary}(i.b) and \ref{fig:summary}(i.c)] and contributes
already at second order, with
\begin{equation}
g_4=\frac{4\Jpm^2}{\Jzz},
\qquad
\frac{g_4}{g_6}=\frac{\Jzz}{3|\Jpm|},
\label{eq:g4}
\end{equation}
whereas the physical hexagon process appears only at third order, at the
scale $g_6$. The artifact therefore diverges relative to the physical process
in the nominally controlled limit $|\Jpm|/\Jzz\to0$. Cubic-16 hosts 36 such
winding four-cycles and 16 contractible hexagons; FCC-32 reduces but does not
remove the problem, with 24 winding four-cycles, 32 contractible hexagons,
and 96 wrapping hexagons. Ordinary ED thus probes the parametrically larger
scale of a finite-torus winding process rather than the ring exchange
governing the emergent gauge sector, and the lower anomaly it produces is
centered near $g_4$ rather than $g_6$, as shown in
Fig.~\ref{fig:summary}(ii).

In this Letter, we remove this artifact by coupling microscopic spin
transfers to an auxiliary source that records their transport on the covering
lattice. We map the exact ice-descended band to a fixed reference space and
retain only its zero-transport Fourier component. This transport-character
projection removes noncontractible winding processes while preserving
contractible hexagon dynamics and microscopic virtual dressing. On cubic-16,
the projected band is converged to sub-percent accuracy with source-grid size
and recovers the correct low-energy thermodynamic scale at a sign-free
benchmark point. The projection also strongly reorganizes the longitudinal
spectrum, demonstrating that the winding artifact affects both energies and
matrix elements.

\emph{Transport-character projection.}---The physical and spurious processes
can be separated without reference to perturbative order. A microscopic
sequence may return to the same spin configuration on the finite torus while
transporting Ising polarization through a periodic boundary: it is closed on
the torus, but fails to close when lifted continuously to the infinite
covering lattice. The winding four-cycles transport nonzero polarization,
whereas a contractible hexagon transports none
[Figs.~\ref{fig:summary}(i.a)--\ref{fig:summary}(i.f)]. Equivalently, each
exchange creates or moves a spinon--antispinon pair, and the sequence returns
to the ice manifold only by annihilating it: on a contractible loop the two
charges genuinely meet, whereas on a winding loop each meets a periodic image
of its partner, which is why the process survives on the torus but has no
counterpart on the covering lattice. We now promote this observation to an
exact operator identity.

A periodic cluster is specified by three period vectors, collected as the
rows of a $3\times3$ matrix $L$, and each site $i$ is assigned a
representative position $\br_i$ in a chosen fundamental cell. For every
stored oriented nearest-neighbor bond $(i,j)$ we choose
$\bn_{ij}\in\mathbb Z^3$ such that the covering-lattice image of $j$
connected directly to $i$ lies at $\bm R_{j|i}=\br_j-\bn_{ij}^{T}L$; thus
$\bn_{ij}=0$ for an intracell bond, whereas $\bn_{ij}\ne0$ records the
periodic image reached by a boundary-crossing bond. The covering-lattice
displacement along the oriented bond and the corresponding pair coordinate
are $\bm d_{ij}=\bm R_{j|i}-\br_i$ and $\bm c_{ij}=\br_i+\bm R_{j|i}$.

To quantify transport, consider a spin configuration $\mathcal C$ lifted from
the torus to the covering lattice, and associate with it the polarization
coordinate
\begin{equation}
\bm p(\mathcal C)
=
\sum_{i=1}^{N} S_i^z(\mathcal C)\,\bm R_i ,
\label{eq:polarization}
\end{equation}
where $i$ runs over all $N$ cluster sites and $\bm R_i$ is the
covering-lattice position assigned to site $i$ by the lift. Equation
(\ref{eq:polarization}) is the first spatial moment of the site-resolved
Ising polarization. It introduces no new physical degree of freedom and is
neither an electric nor a magnetic dipole moment, nor a dipole formed from
the tetrahedral charges $Q_t$; it is a bookkeeping coordinate recording how
$S^z$ is displaced by a sequence of microscopic spin flips.

Taken alone, $\bm p$ is not single valued on a torus: replacing a site by one
of its periodic images shifts it by a period vector. Its change along a
continuously lifted sequence is unambiguous, however, and for an ice-to-ice
sequence is also independent of the coordinate origin, because the ice rule
implies $\sum_i S_i^z=0$. The increments then follow directly from
Eq.~(\ref{eq:polarization}): $S_i^+S_j^-$ moves one unit of $S^z$ from
$\bm R_{j|i}$ to $\br_i$, giving $\Delta\bm p=-\bm d_{ij}$, whereas
$S_i^+S_j^+$ raises both endpoints and displaces $\bm p$ by their summed
position $\bm c_{ij}$. A move $T_\ell$ on the oriented bond $(i,j)$ therefore
contributes
\begin{equation}
\Delta\bm p_\ell=
\begin{cases}
\mp\,\bm d_{ij},
&
T_\ell=S_i^{\pm}S_j^{\mp},
\\[2pt]
\pm\,\bm c_{ij},
&
T_\ell=S_i^{\pm}S_j^{\pm}.
\end{cases}
\label{eq:polarization_increment}
\end{equation}

We introduce an auxiliary counting field $\btheta$ conjugate to these
increments by multiplying each microscopic move by
$e^{-2i\btheta\cdot\Delta\bm p_\ell}$, giving the sourced Hamiltonian
\begin{align}
H(\btheta)
={}&
\Jzz\sum_{\langle ij\rangle}S_i^zS_j^z
-\Jpm\sum_{\langle ij\rangle}
\left(
e^{2i\btheta\cdot\bm d_{ij}}S_i^+S_j^-
+
\mathrm{h.c.}
\right)
\nonumber\\
&+
\Jpp\sum_{\langle ij\rangle}
\left(
e^{-2i\btheta\cdot\bm c_{ij}}S_i^+S_j^+
+
\mathrm{h.c.}
\right).
\label{eq:sourced}
\end{align}
The field $\btheta$ is not a physical electromagnetic field; it is a Fourier
variable that records the polarization transported by microscopic spin
transfers. For any sequence $\gamma$ the source phases multiply to exactly
$e^{-i\btheta\cdot\bq_\gamma}$, with
\begin{equation}
\bq_\gamma=
2\sum_{\ell\in\gamma}\Delta\bm p_\ell ,
\label{eq:transport}
\end{equation}
the factor of two expressing the transport in the natural normalization
$\sigma^z=2S^z=\pm1$. The vector $\bq_\gamma$ is thus the net
Ising-polarization transport of the lifted sequence.

Crucially, $\bq_\gamma$ is a property of the path through configuration
space, not merely of its endpoints on the finite torus. For a contractible
closed process the lifted configuration also closes, the polarization returns
to the same branch, and $\bq_\gamma=0$. A winding process instead returns to
precisely the same finite-cluster configuration while transferring
polarization through a periodic boundary; its lift ends on a different branch
and $\bq_\gamma\ne0$. Equation~(\ref{eq:transport}) is therefore an exact
microscopic winding label, valid beyond perturbation theory and in the
presence of both $\Jpm$ and $\Jpp$.

Let $P_{\btheta}$ be the spectral projector onto the isolated band
adiabatically connected to the spin-ice manifold,
\begin{equation}
P_{\btheta}
=
\frac{1}{2\pi i}
\oint_{\Gamma_{\btheta}}
\left(
z-H(\btheta)
\right)^{-1}
\,dz ,
\qquad
\operatorname{rank}P_{\btheta}
=
\operatorname{rank}\Pice ,
\label{eq:spectral}
\end{equation}
where the contour $\Gamma_{\btheta}$ encloses the selected band and no other
part of the spectrum. Because the band eigenvectors depend on $\btheta$,
their matrix representations cannot be averaged directly. We therefore
identify every sourced band with the same fixed ice-rule reference space
using the polar isometry $W_{\btheta}$, and define the exact band Hamiltonian
in that common basis,
\begin{equation}
W_{\btheta}
=
P_{\btheta}\Pice
\left(
\Pice P_{\btheta}\Pice
\right)^{-1/2},
\qquad
h(\btheta)
=
W_{\btheta}^{\dagger}
H(\btheta)
W_{\btheta}.
\label{eq:polar}
\end{equation}
A continuous source average retains only the zero-transport Fourier
component, which we evaluate numerically on an $M^3$ grid,
\begin{equation}
h_{\rm wf}
=
\frac{1}{(2\pi)^3}
\!\int_{[0,2\pi)^3}\!\!
d^3\btheta\,
h(\btheta)
\;\simeq\;
h_M
=
\frac{1}{M^3}
\sum_{\bm m\in\mathbb Z_M^3}
h\!\left(
\frac{2\pi\bm m}{M}
\right).
\label{eq:character}
\end{equation}
The continuous average selects $\bq_\gamma=0$ exactly, removing every process
that carries nonzero covering-lattice transport; the discrete grid instead
retains all characters with $\bq_\gamma=\bm 0\pmod M$, so convergence in $M$
is an essential diagnostic. One consequence of the continuous average is
available exactly at any $M$: since $\bq_\gamma=2\Delta\bm p$, a surviving
matrix element cannot change $\bm p$, so $h_{\rm wf}$ is block diagonal in the
polarization coordinate of Eq.~(\ref{eq:polarization}). We therefore impose
that block structure directly on $h_M$. The residual inter-sector coupling it
discards is only $1.6\times10^{-5}\Jzz$ at $M=4$, but it is comparable to the
splittings within the low-lying manifold and would otherwise lift
degeneracies that the exact projection protects. The construction ceases to be well defined if the
selected band closes its spectral gap or if $\Pice P_{\btheta}\Pice$ becomes
singular.

For thermodynamics we replace only the exact band descended from the ice
manifold,
\begin{equation}
Z_{\rm wf}(\beta)
=
Z_{\rm full}(\beta)
-
\operatorname{Tr}_{\rm ice}
e^{-\beta h(\bm0)}
+
\operatorname{Tr}_{\rm ice}
e^{-\beta\bar h_M},
\label{eq:replacement}
\end{equation}
and perform the analogous substitution for the first two energy moments that
give the internal energy and heat capacity; the microscopic spectrum outside
the selected band is left unchanged. Here
\begin{equation}
\bar h_M
=
h_M+\frac{\operatorname{Tr}\left[h(\bm0)-h_M\right]}{d}I,
\qquad
d=\operatorname{rank}\Pice ,
\label{eq:trace_match}
\end{equation}
fixes the scalar placement of the corrected band relative to the microscopic
spectrum. The band Hamiltonians of Eq.~(\ref{eq:polar}) are referred to the
ice manifold and therefore carry no absolute energy origin, which the
character average of Eq.~(\ref{eq:character}) does not supply; matching the
band trace to $h(\bm0)$ supplies it. Because a uniform shift of an isolated
band cancels in both $C$ and $S$, this choice leaves the reported
low-temperature observables unchanged. Equation~(\ref{eq:replacement}) defines
a finite-size diagnostic rather than the partition function of the original
periodic Hamiltonian. Derivations and numerical details are given in the
Supplemental Material~\cite{supp}.

\emph{Validation against perturbation theory.}---The projection is defined
nonperturbatively, but wherever the loop expansion is controlled the
projected band must reduce to pure hexagon ring exchange. This is a
quantitative test, because the band Hamiltonian is written in the fixed
reference space spanned by the $d=90$ zero-spinon configurations of cubic-16:
the matrix element between two configurations that differ by the spins of a
given loop is that loop's amplitude, and can be read off and compared with
Eqs.~(\ref{eq:g6}) and (\ref{eq:g4}). We do this at the sign-free benchmark
point
\begin{equation}
(\Jpm,\Jpp)/\Jzz=(0.046,0),
\label{eq:benchmark_point}
\end{equation}
where $g_6=1.168\times10^{-3}\Jzz$ and $g_4=8.464\times10^{-3}\Jzz$.

In the periodic band $h(\bm0)$ each of the 36 winding four-loops carries
$1.046\times10^{-2}\Jzz=1.24\,g_4$, exceeding the contractible hexagon
amplitude $1.314\times10^{-3}\Jzz=1.13\,g_6$ by a factor of $8.0$: the
leading ice-to-ice process of ordinary ED is the artifact, not the ring
exchange. In the projected band $h_{M=4}$ all 36 winding four-loop
amplitudes and all 48 wrapping-hexagon amplitudes vanish,
$|h_{ij}|\le1.2\times10^{-16}\Jzz$, fourteen orders of magnitude below $g_4$
and at the level of the arithmetic. All 16 contractible hexagon amplitudes
survive at $1.325\times10^{-3}\Jzz=1.13\,g_6$, differing from their periodic
values by $0.8\%$. Their $13\%$ excess over bare third order is higher-order
virtual dressing, which the exact-band construction retains by design. The
same audit on FCC-32 removes all 24 winding four-loops and all 96 wrapping
hexagons while retaining all 32 contractible hexagons; the full retention
census for both clusters is given in the Supplemental
Material~\cite{supp}.

Two conditions make the band itself well defined, and both are met. The
selected band is separated from the rest of the $65\,536$-state spectrum by
$0.3874\Jzz$, two orders of magnitude above the scales it controls, so the
contour in Eq.~(\ref{eq:spectral}) is unambiguous; and the smallest overlap
between a band eigenvector and the zero-spinon manifold is $0.762$ across the
source grid, so $\Pice P_{\btheta}\Pice$ stays far from singular and the
polar pullback of Eq.~(\ref{eq:polar}) exists at every source point. Finally,
the source average is converged: after removing the scalar component of the
band Hamiltonian, $h_M$ changes by $6.36\%$ from $M=2$ to $M=3$ but only
$0.013\%$ from $M=3$ to $M=4$. Coarse grids alias transport sectors with
$\bq_\gamma=\bm0\pmod M$ into the zero character; by $M=4$ that aliasing is
exhausted, and we use $M=4$ throughout.

\emph{Thermodynamics and dynamics.}---With the artifact removed we can
compare against QMC. At the benchmark point of
Eq.~(\ref{eq:benchmark_point}),
continuous-time worm QMC on $8^3$ primitive cells \cite{Huang2018} places the
low-temperature heat-capacity maximum at $T/\Jzz\approx0.001100$. Ordinary
cubic-16 ED gives $0.02554$, tracking the four-loop scale $g_4$; the
winding-free replacement gives $0.001463$, tracking $g_6$
[Fig.~\ref{fig:summary}(ii)]. Over the low-temperature window the
heat-capacity root-mean-square error drops from $0.0859$ to $0.02327$, while
the entropy error is essentially unchanged at $0.04509$. The projection thus
fixes the low-energy scale; it does not make a 16-site torus a quantitative
model of the bulk.

The same comparison can be made in the spectrum. To probe the periodic and
projected bands with an identical microscopic operator, we map the spin
operator into the fixed ice reference space with the zero-source isometry
$W_{\bm0}$, defining for sublattice $a$
\begin{equation}
\widetilde S_a^z(\bk)
=
W_{\bm0}^{\dagger}
\left(
\sum_{j\in a}
e^{-2\pi i\bk\cdot\br_j}
S_j^z
\right)
W_{\bm0},
\label{eq:dssf_source}
\end{equation}
where $\bk$ is the reciprocal-space momentum, distinct from the transport
label $\bq_\gamma$. For either the periodic band Hamiltonian $h(\bm0)$ or the
projected Hamiltonian $h_{M=4}$, the zero-temperature longitudinal structure
factor is
\begin{align}
S^{zz}(\bk,\omega)
={}&
\frac{1}{N d_0}
\sum_{g\in\mathcal G}
\sum_{n\notin\mathcal G}
\sum_{a=0}^{3}
\left|
\left\langle
n
\left|
\widetilde S_a^z(\bk)
\right|
g
\right\rangle
\right|^2
\nonumber\\
&\times
G_\eta
\left[
\omega-(E_n-E_g)
\right],
\label{eq:dssf}
\end{align}
where $\mathcal G$ is the ground-state manifold, $d_0=|\mathcal G|$, $N$ is
the number of sites, $\eta=6\times10^{-4}\Jzz$ is the spectral broadening,
and $|g\rangle$, $|n\rangle$ are eigenstates of the band Hamiltonian under
consideration.

Cubic-16 resolves only $\Gamma$ and three symmetry-related $X$ points. At
$X$, the spectral peak shifts from $0.08069\Jzz$ in the periodic band to
$0.005362\Jzz$ after projection [Figs.~\ref{fig:summary}(iii.a) and
\ref{fig:summary}(iii.b)]. Since $X$ sits on the zone boundary, this is
directly comparable to the photon bandwidth $W_\gamma\approx5\times10^{-3}
\Jzz$ extracted from QMC with stochastic analytic continuation at the same
coupling \cite{Huang2018}: the projected peak agrees to $7\%$, while the
periodic band overshoots sixteenfold. At $\Gamma$ the projected response
vanishes identically, because the $\bk=\bm0$ probe is the sublattice
magnetization, a c-number on each polarization block; the QMC-SAC spectrum
likewise carries all of its $\bq=\bm0$ weight at zero energy \cite{Huang2018}.
The projection therefore reorganizes excitation energies and spectral weights
together, rather than rescaling the periodic spectrum.

One artifact of exact polarization conservation should be noted: the
projected ground state is sixfold degenerate, the sectors
$\bm p=(\pm\tfrac12,0,0)$, $(0,\pm\tfrac12,0)$, $(0,0,\pm\tfrac12)$ being
unable to tunnel into one another. The resulting $\ln6$ per cluster is a
floor under the low-temperature entropy on cubic-16, and accounts for part of
the residual entropy discrepancy, but the sector count grows only
polynomially with linear size and so vanishes per site in the thermodynamic
limit.

\emph{Discussion.}---Periodic ED replaces the physical hexagon dynamics of
QSI with a parametrically larger noncontractible four-spin process.
Transport-character projection removes it from the exact ice-descended band
while preserving contractible processes, microscopic virtual dressing, and
the spectrum outside the band. Read directly in the zero-spinon basis, the
projected band has no winding amplitude left at the $10^{-16}$ level and
retains every hexagon at the ring-exchange scale; with that established, the
corrected cluster reproduces both the QMC heat-capacity scale and the QMC
photon bandwidth at the sign-free point. What remains is genuine
finite-size error: cubic-16 has no long-wavelength photon sector and sparse
momentum resolution, so the projection is a controlled finite-torus
correction, not a substitute for thermodynamic extrapolation.

The method is sign unrestricted, so the natural next step is a
character-converged FCC-32 calculation at the $\pi$-flux XYZ parameters
relevant to Ce$_2$Hf$_2$O$_7$, where QMC cannot follow.
