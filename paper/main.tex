\title{Unbiased exact diagonalization of quantum spin ice in the
$\pi$-flux regime:\\
removing finite-size loops by translationally-invariant twist averaging}
\author{}
\date{\today}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
The thermodynamics and dynamics of quantum spin ice (QSI) are governed
by an emergent compact $U(1)$ gauge field. Its unfrustrated
\emph{$0$-flux} sector ($\Jpm>0$) is accessible to sign-problem-free
quantum Monte Carlo (QMC); the frustrated \emph{$\pi$-flux} sector
($\Jpm<0$) realized in the dipolar--octupolar cerium pyrochlores carries
a QMC sign problem, leaving exact diagonalization (ED) as the only
unbiased finite-temperature method. ED has a finite-size problem
specific to QSI: a periodic cluster hosts short ice-preserving ring
exchanges that wind the torus and have no infinite-lattice analogue. The
dominant ones are four-site loops with coupling $\gfour=4\Jpm^2/\Jzz$,
which is \emph{second} order in $\Jpm$ and hence parametrically larger
than the genuine photon coupling $\ghex=12|\Jpm|^3/\Jzz^2$; they
contaminate exactly the emergent-gauge observables (the low-$T$
specific-heat peak and the low-energy $S^{zz}(\bq,\omega)$). We remove
them by twist averaging in the translationally-invariant gauge:
threading a $U(1)$ flux through the cluster as a uniform vector potential
on \emph{every} bond and averaging the effective Hamiltonian over the
flux. The winding loops offset the magnetization ($S^z$) pattern across
the cluster, so the flux dephases them, while genuine bulk ring
exchanges -- balanced, with no such offset -- are untouched. The average
works, but only if three conditions the naive procedure violates are met:
the gauge must be uniform (the boundary-only gauge removes merely half),
the average must span the doubled period set by the half-integer $S^z$
dipole (the naive $2\pi$ leaves a $40\%$ residual), and it must act on
the Hamiltonian rather than on an observable. On the $16$- and $32$-site
clusters this moves the low-$T$ gauge peak and the low-energy $S^{zz}$
weight from the artifact scale $\gfour$ to the photon scale $\ghex$ for
both flux signs, while leaving the high-$T$ charge (spinon) peak --
genuine, uncontaminated physics -- untouched. This makes a $32$-site
pyrochlore an unbiased laboratory for the $\pi$-flux gauge sector, to be
benchmarked against $0$-flux QMC and applied at the exchange parameters
of the cerium pyrochlores.
\end{abstract}

\section{The problem}
\label{sec:problem}

\paragraph{Quantum spin ice and its emergent photon.}
The minimal model of QSI is the XXZ antiferromagnet on the pyrochlore
lattice, in the local $\langle111\rangle$ frames,
\begin{equation}
H=\Jzz\sum_{\langle ij\rangle}S^z_iS^z_j
-\Jpm\sum_{\langle ij\rangle}\bigl(S^+_iS^-_j+S^-_iS^+_j\bigr),
\qquad 0<|\Jpm|\ll\Jzz .
\label{eq:H}
\end{equation}
For $\Jpm\to0$ the ground manifold is the exponentially degenerate set
of $\twoinout$ (ice-rule) states. A small $\Jpm$ lifts the degeneracy
through virtual ring exchanges and generates the deconfined $U(1)$
lattice gauge theory of QSI: $S^z$ is the emergent electric field, the
third-order hexagonal ring exchange
\begin{equation}
\ghex=\frac{12\,|\Jpm|^3}{\Jzz^2}
\label{eq:ghex}
\end{equation}
is the Maxwell term whose quantum is the emergent photon, and ice-rule
defects are gapped, fractionalized spinons (pair threshold
$\Ds\approx0.8\,\Jzz$ on the clusters below). The \emph{sign} of the
ring coupling follows $\mathrm{sgn}(\Jpm^3)$: $\Jpm>0$ gives the
unfrustrated $0$-flux vacuum, $\Jpm<0$ the frustrated $\pi$-flux vacuum.
Thermodynamically the photon appears as a low-$T$ Schottky peak in
$C(T)$ at $T\sim\ghex$, far below the spinon (charge) peak at
$T\sim\Jzz$; dynamically it appears as low-energy weight in the
non-spin-flip channel $S^{zz}(\bq,\omega)$.

\paragraph{Why $\pi$-flux needs exact diagonalization.}
In the $S^z$ basis the off-diagonal matrix elements of \cref{eq:H} are
$-\Jpm$. For $\Jpm>0$ they are negative and worldline/stochastic-series
QMC is sign-problem-free: the $0$-flux QSI is quantitatively
characterized. For $\Jpm<0$ they are positive and QMC acquires a sign
problem. The $\pi$-flux regime -- believed to describe
Ce$_2$Zr$_2$O$_7$, Ce$_2$Sn$_2$O$_7$, Ce$_2$Hf$_2$O$_7$ -- is thus a QMC
dead zone, and unbiased finite-$T$ information must come from exact
diagonalization of a finite cluster.

\paragraph{The finite-size problem.}
A periodic cluster is a flat torus, and its periodic identifications
generate ice-preserving ring exchanges that \emph{wind} the torus with
no counterpart on the infinite lattice. The shortest and most damaging
are \emph{four-site} loops. Because a four-site loop factorizes into two
disjoint nearest-neighbour (NN) hops, it is generated at \emph{second}
order in $\Jpm$,
\begin{equation}
\gfour=\frac{4\,\Jpm^2}{\Jzz},
\qquad\text{so}\qquad
\frac{\gfour}{\ghex}=\frac{\Jzz}{3|\Jpm|}\gg1 .
\label{eq:g4}
\end{equation}
The artifact is thus \emph{parametrically larger} than the physics, and
grows as $|\Jpm|\to0$ -- the weak-coupling regime of the cerium
materials. It lives entirely in the ice manifold, so it contaminates
precisely the emergent-gauge observables: it pushes the low-$T$ $C(T)$
peak and the low-energy $S^{zz}$ weight up to the scale $\gfour$ instead
of $\ghex$ (quantified in \cref{sec:demo}). The spinon continuum
($S^{+-}$ at $\omega\sim\Jzz$) lies outside the ice manifold and is
\emph{not} contaminated.

\paragraph{Goal.}
Remove this finite-size contamination from the gauge sector of a
periodic-cluster ED, so that the $32$-site pyrochlore delivers unbiased
$\pi$-flux thermodynamics and dynamical structure factor.

\section{The effective gauge Hamiltonian and the winding loops}
\label{sec:setup}

\paragraph{Cluster torus.}
The cluster carries $N$ sites with periodic identifications generated by
the rows of an integer matrix $\bL\in\Z^{3\times3}$ (in units of the
cubic lattice constant $a$); position space is the flat torus
$T^3=\mathbb R^3/\bL\Z^3$ with first homology $\Z^3$. Each NN bond
$(i,j)$ has a \emph{wrap vector}
$\bn_{ij}=\mathrm{round}[(\br_j-\br_i)\bL^{-1}]$, zero in the interior
and nonzero across the boundary.

\paragraph{Effective Hamiltonian.}
Degenerate ($\Jpm$-)perturbation theory in the ice manifold yields
$\Heff=\sum_{\mathcal C}c_{\mathcal C}O_{\mathcal C}$, a sum of
ring-exchange operators
$O_{\mathcal C}=(S^+_aS^-_bS^+_cS^-_d\cdots)$ over ice-preserving cycles
$\mathcal C$; because each intermediate resolvent is Ising
($\propto\Jzz$), $c_{\mathcal C}\propto\Jpm^{|\mathcal C|/2}$ exactly. On
the $2{\times}2{\times}2$ FCC cluster ($N=32$) the ice manifold has
dimension $2970$, so $\Heff$ is \emph{fully diagonalizable} and its
thermodynamics is numerically exact. Enumeration (Schrieffer--Wolff with
per-process bookkeeping) gives \cref{tab:census}: the winding four-loops
and wrapping hexagons are the contamination; the contractible hexagons
carry the photon.

\section{The method: twist averaging in the translationally-invariant gauge}
\label{sec:method}

\paragraph{The label that separates artifact from physics.}
The winding loops share one feature that no genuine bulk ring exchange
has: they offset the magnetization ($S^z$) pattern \emph{across} the
cluster. Define the $S^z$ dipole of a ring exchange $O_{\mathcal C}$
(which raises a site set $R$, lowers a set $L$) as
\begin{equation}
\bdelta_{\mathcal C}=\brho_{\mathcal C}+\bN\!\cdot\!\bL,
\qquad
\brho_{\mathcal C}=\sum_{i\in R}\br_i-\sum_{j\in L}\br_j ,
\label{eq:delta}
\end{equation}
the net spatial displacement of the flipped $S^z$: $\brho$ is the
intrinsic dipole (home positions), $\bN\!\cdot\!\bL$ the boundary-wrap
contribution. (This is the microscopic $S^z$ associated with
$S^z_{\rm tot}$ conservation -- to which the twist flux below couples --
\emph{not} the emergent electric charge; the ice manifold is the
charge-free vacuum, and $O_{\mathcal C}$ creates no spinons. The net
$S^z_{\rm tot}$ is conserved, so $\bdelta$ measures whether the process's
$S^z$ current \emph{winds} the torus.) A contractible hexagon closes in
real space and is balanced, $\bdelta=\bm0$; a winding four-loop closes
only through the boundary, so its flipped spins cannot balance and its
$S^z$ pattern is offset by half a cell, $\bdelta\neq\bm0$
(\cref{fig:transport}). A twist flux dephases exactly the loops with
$\bdelta\neq\bm0$.

\begin{figure}[t]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figs/fig_transport.pdf}
\caption{The $S^z$ dipole of a ring exchange on the $16$-site cubic
pyrochlore cell, $\bdelta=\sum_{\rm raised}\br-\sum_{\rm lowered}\br$.
(a) A contractible hexagon (sites $0,2,7,4,14,15$) is balanced -- raised

\paragraph{The average.}
Thread a $U(1)$ flux $\bvarphi$ through the cluster as a uniform vector
potential $\bA=\bL^{-1}\bvarphi$ on \emph{every} bond
($\theta_{ij}=\bA\cdot\bd_{ij}$, $\bd_{ij}$ the minimum-image
displacement), so that each ring exchange picks up the phase of its
$S^z$ dipole, $c_{\mathcal C}\to c_{\mathcal C}\,e^{-i\bA\cdot\bdelta_{\mathcal C}}$,
and average the \emph{Hamiltonian} over the flux:
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\;\;
\overline{\Heff}=\int\Heff(\bvarphi)\,\frac{d\bvarphi}{(\text{period})^3},
\qquad
c_{\mathcal C}\to c_{\mathcal C}\,\overline{e^{-i\bvarphi\cdot(\bL^{-1}\bdelta_{\mathcal C})}}
=c_{\mathcal C}\,\big[\bdelta_{\mathcal C}\in\bL\Z^3\big].
\;\;}
\label{eq:avg}
\end{equation}
The physical loop ($\bdelta=\bm0$) is kept; the winding loop
($\bdelta\neq\bm0$) is dephased away, at every order in $\Jpm$. This is
the familiar twist-averaging idea, and it works -- but only if three
conditions the naive procedure violates are met:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] \emph{Uniform gauge.} The phase must sit on every bond. If the
flux sits only on the boundary bonds, a winding loop has a virtual path
through interior bonds alone that carries no phase and survives, leaving
\emph{half} the coupling.
\item[(ii)] \emph{Doubled period.} A winding loop offsets its $S^z$
pattern by half a lattice vector ($\bL^{-1}\brho=\tfrac12$; verified for
all winding four-loops and wrapping hexagons on both clusters), so its
phase $e^{-i\bvarphi/2}$ is periodic only modulo $4\pi$. Averaging over
the naive $[0,2\pi)^3$ leaves a $\sim\!40\%$ residual; the period
$[0,4\pi)^3$ is required.
\item[(iii)] \emph{Operator level.} One must average $\Heff$, then
diagonalize. Averaging a specific observable $C(T)$ or $S(\bq,\omega)$
instead keeps the spurious scale in any gauge.
\end{itemize}

\section{Demonstration}
\label{sec:demo}

The average gives the winding four-loop coefficient (and the resulting
low-$T$ specific-heat peak) shown here, at $\Jpm=-0.1\,\Jzz$ on the
$16$-site cluster, isolating each of the three conditions:
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lcc}
\toprule
scheme & winding coeff.\ $/\gfour$ & $\Tpk/\ghex$ \\
\midrule
none (bare) & $-1.00$ & $3.18$ \\
boundary gauge (violates (i)) & $-0.50$ & $1.63$ \\
smooth gauge, $[0,2\pi)^3$ (violates (ii)) & $+0.40$ & $0.84$ \\
smooth gauge, $[0,4\pi)^3$ (correct) & $0.00$ & $0.62$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
Only the uniform gauge over the doubled period removes the loop
($0/0/1$ survival of \cref{tab:census}, to machine precision, with the
contractible hexagon kept at $+\ghex$). \Cref{fig:ct} shows the full
$C(T)$ from exact $16$-site diagonalization ($12870$ states): the cleaned
low-$T$ gauge (photon) peak sits at the physical scale $\ghex$, while the
high-$T$ charge (spinon) peak at $T\sim\Jzz/3$ -- genuine physics outside
the ice manifold -- is untouched, as it must be. On the $32$-site FCC
cluster (exact full spectrum of the $2970$-state ice manifold) the bare
gauge peak sits at the artifact scale and drifts up as
$\gfour/\ghex=\Jzz/3|\Jpm|$ toward weak coupling, while the average holds
it at $O(\ghex)$; the same holds for the low-$\omega$ weight of
$S^{zz}(\bq,\omega)$ on both clusters and both flux signs.
